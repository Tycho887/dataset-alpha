# ETSI TS 119 172-1 V1.1.1: Electronic Signatures and Infrastructures (ESI); Signature Policies; Part 1: Building blocks and table of contents for human readable signature policy documents
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V1.1.1 | **Date**: July 2015 | **Type**: Normative  
**Original**: [http://www.etsi.org/standards-search](http://www.etsi.org/standards-search)

## Scope (Summary)
Defines building blocks of signature policy and specifies a table of contents for human readable signature policy documents.

## Definitions and Abbreviations
### Definitions
- **CA-certificate**: public-key certificate for one CA issued by another CA or by the same CA (as per ITU-T X.509)
- **certification authority (CA)**: authority trusted to create and assign public-key certificates
- **certification path**: ordered list of public-key certificates starting with a trust anchor and ending with the certificate to be validated
- **certificate validation**: process of verifying that a certificate is valid
- **cryptographic system**: collection of transformations from plain text to cipher text and vice versa
- **data integrity**: property that data has not been altered or destroyed in an unauthorized manner
- **data origin authentication**: corroboration that the source of data received is as claimed
- **digital signature**: data appended to or cryptographic transformation of a data unit allowing proof of source and integrity
- **private key**: key of an entity's key pair known only by that entity
- **public key**: key of an entity's key pair which is publicly known
- **public key certificate**: public key of an entity rendered unforgeable by digital signature of the issuing CA
- **public key infrastructure**: infrastructure supporting management of public keys for authentication, encryption, integrity or non-repudiation
- **repudiation**: denial by an entity of having participated in a communication
- **signature augmentation**: process of incorporating information to maintain signature validity over the long term
- **signature augmentation policy**: set of rules for augmentation of digital signatures to meet a business need
- **signature creation device**: configured software or hardware used to create a digital signature
- **signature creation policy**: set of rules for creation of digital signatures
- **signature policy**: signature creation policy, augmentation policy, validation policy or any combination thereof
- **signature policy authority**: entity responsible for drafting, registering, maintaining, issuing and updating a signature policy
- **signature policy document**: document expressing one or more signature policies in human readable form
- **signature validation**: process of verifying that a digital signature is valid
- **signature validation policy**: set of rules for validation of digital signatures
- **trust**: firm belief in reliability and truth of information or ability of an entity to act appropriately
- **Trust Anchor (TA)**: entity trusted by a relying party for validating certificates
- **Trust Anchor information**: distinguished name, associated public key, algorithm identifier, parameters, constraints and validity period
- **validation data**: data used to validate a digital signature

## 4 Signature policies and signature policy document
- A signature policy **shall** be derived from analysis of requirements applicable to the implementation of digital signatures into a specific business electronic process or application domain.
- The resulting rules **shall** be documented in a signature policy.
- A signature policy **shall** cover at least one of: signature creation policy, signature augmentation policy, or signature validation policy.
- When expressing a signature policy in human readable form, the table of content (ToC) specified in annex A **shall** be followed to establish the corresponding signature policy document, or the signature policy **shall** be expressed under the form of a signature policy statement summary established on the basis of table A.1.
- The numbering of clauses **shall** appear as in annex A by removing the starting "A.". Each clause **shall** appear; if not applicable, "not applicable" **shall** be written after the clause title.
- The text provided in each clause of annex A **shall not** be copied.
- Where applicable, sub-clauses may identify separate provisions for creation, augmentation and validation using labels [CREATION], [AUGMENTATION], [VALIDATION].
- Provisions may be texted explicitly or incorporated by reference.
- Clause A.3 covers rules set by a signature policy organized against Business Scoping Parameters (BSPs).
- The sub-clauses of clause A.3 **shall** each include the description of the applicable BSP provisions in business language and **shall** indicate separately the corresponding requirements on signers, entities augmenting signatures and/or relying parties.
- When several groups of signatures are addressed, a single signature policy document may be used.
- The signature policy document **shall** be provided in the form of a PDF/A-2 document. It **shall** be digitally signed according to PAdES baseline signatures.

## Annex A (normative): Table of contents for signature policies expressed as human readable documents
### A.1 Introduction
#### A.1.1 Overview
This clause **shall** provide a general introduction to the document being written. It **shall** provide a synopsis of the business or application domain and the specific process to which the signature policy(ies) applies.

#### A.1.2 Business or Application Domain
##### A.1.2.1 Scope and boundaries of signature policy
This clause **shall** describe the scope and boundaries of the business (application) domain in which the signature policy(ies) is(are) suitable for use. When applicable, the hierarchy of signature policies **shall** be detailed and illustrated.

##### A.1.2.2 Domain of applications
When applicable, this clause **shall** further describe each domain of applications.

##### A.1.2.3 Transactional context
This clause **shall** provide additional information about the transactional context, when applicable.

#### A.1.3 Document and policy(ies) names, identification and conformance rules
##### A.1.3.1 Signature policy document and signature policy(ies) names
This clause **shall** provide information about any applicable name for the signature policy document and for the signature policy(ies) covered.

##### A.1.3.2 Signature policy document and signature policy(ies) identifier(s)
This clause **shall** provide information about identifiers. At least one distinct unique identifier **shall** be allocated to the signature policy document itself and to each signature policy it covers.

##### A.1.3.3 Conformance rules
This clause **shall** provide information about conformance rules.

##### A.1.3.4 Distribution points
This clause **shall** provide information about where the signature policy document is available.

#### A.1.4 Signature policy document administration
##### A.1.4.1 Signature policy authority
This clause **shall** include the name, country, address, and registration number of the signature policy authority. It **shall** also provide information identifying the public key certificate used to digitally sign the document. When the authority determines subordination of policies, additional contact details and procedures **shall** be included.

##### A.1.4.2 Contact person
When the contact point is a natural person, this clause **shall** include name, email address, and telephone number. In other cases, it **shall** include a title or role, email alias, and generalized contact information.

##### A.1.4.3 Approval procedures
This clause **shall** include the procedures by which approval of the signature policy document is made.

#### A.1.5 Definitions and Acronyms
This clause **shall** contain a list or reference to a list of definitions and acronyms used.

### A.2 Signature application practices statements
This clause **shall** include, either by reference or explicitly, the set of policy and security practices requirements that the driving application (DA), signature creation application (SCA) and/or signature validation application (SVA) **shall** meet. When included explicitly, the table of content **shall** conform to the structure defined in annex D.

### A.3 Business scoping parameters
#### A.3.1 BSPs mainly related to the concerned application/business process
##### A.3.1.1 BSP (a): Workflow (sequencing and timing) of signatures
This clause **shall** describe and specify whether the process addresses a single signature or a set of signatures. When a set, it **shall** describe the workflow. The workflow **shall** indicate: sequence flow, sequencing/cardinality (parallel, serial, counter signatures), application on individual or block of transactions, actors and their roles, sequence of signatures per DTBS, and whether validation is required before next signature. It **shall** indicate whether timing of generation and validation is relevant, and timing constraints. It **shall** indicate whether mass signing is applicable.

##### A.3.1.2 BSP (b): Data to be signed
This clause **shall** describe and specify format of the data to be signed and, where structured, indication whether whole or parts will be signed.

##### A.3.1.3 BSP (c): The relationship between signed data and signature(s)
This clause **shall** describe and specify: need for referencing mechanisms, number of data per signature, relative position (associated, enveloped, enveloping, detached), and signature format/levels.

##### A.3.1.4 BSP (d): Targeted community
This clause **shall** identify and describe the addressed community and any specific community rules.

##### A.3.1.5 BSP (e): Allocation of responsibility for signature validation and augmentation
This clause **shall** describe the allocation of responsibility among relying parties, validation trust services, or counter-signing parties. It should also identify requirements for augmenting signatures.

#### A.3.2 BSPs mainly influenced by legal/regulatory provisions
##### A.3.2.1 BSP (f): Legal type of the signatures
This clause **shall** describe and specify the signature legal type required.

##### A.3.2.2 BSP (g): Commitment assumed by the signer
This clause **shall** specify the authorized types of commitment. A commitment type **shall** be expressed as a unique identifier (OID or URI) associated with a description in UK English. The commitment types defined in annex B may be used.

##### A.3.2.3 BSP (h): Level of assurance on timing evidences
This clause **shall** describe and specify requirements on timing evidence level of assurance. It should address whether timing evidence is required and whether claimed assertions or time stamps from TSPs are required with associated LoA.

##### A.3.2.4 BSP (i): Formalities of signing
This clause **shall** describe and specify how evidences are built with regards to expression of will. It **shall** identify requirements for WYSIWYS environment, proper advice and information, user interface requirements, and correct validation/archival procedures.

##### A.3.2.5 BSP (j): Longevity and resilience to change
This clause **shall** describe and specify the expected longevity and resilience to change of the signature.

##### A.3.2.6 BSP (k): Archival
This clause **shall** describe and specify archival requirements.

#### A.3.3 BSPs mainly related to the actors involved
##### A.3.3.1 BSP (l): Identity (and roles/attributes) of the signers
This clause **shall** describe and specify requirements on nature/type of signers, identification rules, roles/attributes, and proof of authority. It **shall** identify necessary components for signature attribution and required identity attributes.

##### A.3.3.2 BSP (m): Level of assurance required for the authentication of the signer
This clause **shall** describe and specify the level of assurance required for signer authentication.

##### A.3.3.3 BSP (n): Signature creation devices
This clause **shall** describe and specify requirements on signature creation devices.

#### A.3.4 Other BSPs
##### A.3.4.1 BSP (o): Other information to be associated with the signature
This clause **shall** describe and specify requirements to associate other information with the signature.

##### A.3.4.2 BSP (p): Cryptographic suites
This clause **shall** describe and specify requirements on robustness of cryptographic suites. Guidance from ETSI TS 119 312 should be taken into account.

##### A.3.4.3 BSP (q): Technological environment
This clause **shall** identify constraints on technologies for signature creation and validation applications.

### A.4 Requirements / statements on technical mechanisms and standards implementation
#### A.4.1 Technical counterparts of BSPs - Statement summary
For each signature identified, this clause **shall** summarize the requirements and specify the corresponding technical mechanisms and standards. It **shall** specify the selected signature format(s) including details. This clause should use the signature policy statement summary table (Table A.1) per signature or group of signatures.

#### A.4.2 Input and output constraints for signature creation, augmentation and validation procedures
##### A.4.2.1 Input constraints to be used when generating, augmenting and/or validating signatures
This clause **shall** specify the requirements derived from BSPs on input of creation, augmentation and validation procedures. It should use Table A.2 per concerned signature. Table A.2 includes constraints per BSP with constraint values at creation, augmentation, and validation. The following constraints are defined:

- **BSP (a)**: OrderInSequence, SequencingNature (mandated-independent, mandated-serial, mandated-unsigned-properties-counter-signature), TimingRelevance (on sequencing and evidence), MassSigningAcceptable (boolean)
- **BSP (b)**: ConstraintOnDTBS, ContentRelatedConstraintsAsPartOfSignatureElements (e.g., DataObjetFormat, content-hints, content-reference, content-identifier), DOTBSAsAWholeOrInParts (whole/parts)
- **BSP (c)**: BulkSigningRelevance (mandated/prohibited), ConstraintsOnTheNumberOfDOTBS, SignatureRelativePosition (associated/enveloped/enveloping/detached), MandatedSignatureFormat
- **BSP (d)**: TargetedCommunityConstraints
- **BSP (e)**: ValidationRequiredBeforeAugmenting (boolean), AugmentToLevel
- **BSP (f)**: ConstraintsOnCertificateMetadata (LegalPersonSignerRequired, LegalPersonSignerAllowed)
- **BSP (g)**: CommitmentTypesRequired (MandatedSignedQProperties-commitment-type-indication, MandatedCommitmentTypeValues with MatchingValuesIndicator and values)
- **BSP (h)**: LoAOnTimingEvidences (LoA-on-signing-time, content-time-stamp, signature-time-stamp, archival-time-stamp, time-in-OCSP-response, time-in-CRL)
- **BSP (i)**: WYSIWYSRequired, WYSIWHBSRequired, ProperAdviceAndInformationRequired, UserInterfaceDesignConstraints, CorrectValidationAndArchivalProcedures
- **BSP (j)**: LoAOnLongevityAndResilience
- **BSP (k)**: ArchivalConstraints
- **BSP (l)**: ConstraintsOnCertificateMetadata (see (f)), MandatedSignedQProperties-signer-attributes, NameConstraints
- **BSP (m)**: X509CertificateValidationConstraints (SetOfTrustAnchors, CertificationPath, user-initial-policy-set, initial-policy-mapping-inhibit, initial-explicit-policy, initial-any-policy-inhibit, initial-permitted-subtrees, initial-excluded-subtrees, path-length-constraints, policy-constraints), RevocationConstraints (RevocationCheckingConstraints with values clrCheck/ocspCheck/bothCheck/eitherCheck/noCheck, RevocationFreshnessConstraints, RevocationInfoOnExpiredCerts), LoAOnTSPPractices
- **BSP (n)**: LoAOnSCD
- **BSP (o)**: MandatedSignedQProperties-signer-location, MandatedUnsignedQProperties-signature-policy-extension, MandatedUnsignedQProperties-signature-policy-inclusion-in-archival-form
- **BSP (p)**: CryptographicSuitesConstraints (Table A.3 with columns: Type of signature, Algorithm identifiers, Minimum signature key size, Minimum length of hash value, Expiration date)
- **BSP (q)**: TechnologicalEnvironmentConstraints

##### A.4.2.2 Output constraints for validating signatures
This clause **shall** specify the requirements on output of validation procedure using Table A.4 per concerned signature.

##### A.4.2.3 Output constraints for generating/augmenting signatures
This clause **shall** specify the requirements on output of creation/augmentation procedure using Table A.5 per concerned signature.

### A.5 Other business and legal matters
This clause **shall** describe general business and legal matters not covered elsewhere, such as: consent, audience conditions, fees, financial responsibility, confidentiality, privacy, IPR, warranties, disclaimers, liability, indemnities, term, notices, amendments, dispute resolution, governing law, compliance, and miscellaneous.

### A.6 Compliance audit and other assessments
This clause **shall** indicate whether applications claiming compliance are required to pass a compliance audit and/or other assessments, and whether determination of subordination requires assessment. It **shall** describe: list of topics, frequency, identity/qualifications of assessors, relationship, actions on deficiencies, and disclosure of results.

## Annex B (normative): Commitment types
The following generic commitment types are defined:
1. **Proof of origin**: signer recognizes to have created, approved and sent the signed data. OID: id-cti-ets-proofOfOrigin (1.2.840.113549.1.9.16.6.1); URI: http://uri.etsi.org/01903/v1.2.2#ProofOfOrigin
2. **Proof of receipt**: signer recognizes to have received the content. OID: id-cti-ets-proofOfReceipt (1.2.840.113549.1.9.16.6.2); URI: http://uri.etsi.org/01903/v1.2.2#ProofOfReceipt
3. **Proof of delivery**: TSP has delivered signed data to local store accessible to recipient. OID: id-cti-ets-proofOfDelivery (1.2.840.113549.1.9.16.6.3); URI: http://uri.etsi.org/01903/v1.2.2#ProofOfDelivery
4. **Proof of sender**: entity has sent the signed data (not necessarily created). OID: id-cti-ets-proofOfSender (1.2.840.113549.1.9.16.6.3); URI: http://uri.etsi.org/01903/v1.2.2#ProofOfSender
5. **Proof of approval**: signer has approved the content. OID: id-cti-ets-proofOfApproval (1.2.840.113549.1.9.16.6.5); URI: http://uri.etsi.org/01903/v1.2.2#ProofOfApproval
6. **Proof of creation**: signer has created the signed data. OID: id-cti-ets-proofOfCreation (1.2.840.113549.1.9.16.6.6); URI: http://uri.etsi.org/01903/v1.2.2#ProofOfCreation

Any organization may create its own URIs/OIDs.

## Annex C (normative): Constraints in the context of EU legislation
The following constraints indicate requirements on certificate metadata under EU legislation:
- **EUQualifiedCertificateRequired**: boolean – signer's certificate must be a qualified certificate as defined in applicable EU legislation.
- **EUQualifiedCertificateSigRequired**: boolean – must be a qualified certificate for electronic signature as defined in [i.1].
- **EUQualifiedCertificateSealRequired**: boolean – must be a qualified certificate for electronic seal as defined in [i.1].
- **EUSSCDRequired**: boolean – private key must reside in a secure signature creation device as defined in [i.1].
- **EUAdESigRequired**: boolean – signature must be an advanced electronic signature as defined in applicable EU legislation.
- **EUAdESealRequired**: boolean – signature must be an advanced electronic seal as defined in [i.1].
- **EUQSigCDRequired**: boolean – private key must reside in a qualified signature creation device as defined in [i.1].
- **EUQSealCDRequired**: boolean – private key must reside in a qualified seal creation device as defined in [i.1].

## Annex D (normative): Signature application practices statements
### D.1 General requirements
When policy and security practices requirements for SCA/SVA are included explicitly in the signature policy document (see clause A.2), the table of content **shall** be as specified in clause D.2 (with numbering obtained by removing starting "D."). This clause **shall** describe a set of rules applicable to the application and its environment. It **shall** include, either by reference or explicitly, the set of policy and security practices requirements that the SCA and/or SVA **shall** meet.

### D.2 Signature application practices statements
#### D.2.1 Legal driven policy requirements
This clause **shall** contain requirements, control objectives and controls in connection with: processing of personal data, significance of digital signatures, and business continuity.

#### D.2.2 Information security (management system) requirements
This clause **shall** contain requirements, control objectives and controls in connection with: security policy(ies), network protection, information system protection, software integrity, data storage security, and audit trail security. (May reference ISO/IEC 27001 [i.6] and 27002 [i.7].)

#### D.2.3 Signature Creation and Signature Validation processes requirements
This clause **shall** contain requirements, control objectives and controls in connection with:
1. Signature creation process: data content type management, signature attribute viewer, timing and sequencing enforcement, signature invocation, selection of signature longevity, signer authentication, DTBS preparation and representation, signature creation device management, communication protection, robustness of cryptographic suites, community adaptability, bulk signing operation.
2. Signature validation process: validation rules enforcement, validation user interface, appropriate signature format, lifetime of signature, validation input/output conformance.

#### D.2.4 Development & coding policy requirements
This clause **shall** contain requirements, control objectives and controls in connection with: secure development methods and testing compliance/interoperability.

#### D.2.5 General requirements
This clause **shall** contain other general requirements, control objectives and controls in connection with: user interface, interface to external trust service providers, and general security measures.
