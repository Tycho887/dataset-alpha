# ETSI TS 119 472-1: Profiles for Electronic Attestation of Attributes; Part 1: General requirements
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V1.2.1 | **Date**: 2026-02 | **Type**: Normative  
**Original**: ETSI TS 119 472-1 V1.2.1 (2026-02)

## Scope (Summary)
Specifies a data model (semantics) for Electronic Attestations of Attributes (EAA) as defined in Regulation (EU) 2024/1183. Defines general requirements for EU Qualified EAA (QEAA) and EU EAA issued by/on behalf of a public body (PuB-EAA). Defines four realizations: SD-JWT VC, ISO/IEC-mdoc, JSON-LD W3C-VC, and X.509-AC, each with specific requirements.

## Definitions and Abbreviations (Selected)
- **EAA administrative validity period**: dates from/up to which attributes are valid (as attribute in attestation)
- **EAA technical validity period**: dates/times from/up to which the EAA is valid
- **Electronic Attestation of Attributes context**: additional info for relying party to process EAA
- **PuB-EAA**: EAA issued by a public body responsible for an authentic source per eIDAS2 Annex VII
- **EAAP**: tamper-proof presentation with cryptographic verification
- **QEAA**: Qualified Electronic Attestation of Attributes per eIDAS2 Annex V
- **SD-JWT VC EAA, ISO/IEC-mdoc EAA, JSON-LD W3C-VC EAA, X.509-AC EAA**: respective realizations
- **Requirement identifiers**: `EAA-`, `QEAA-`, `PuB-EAA-` followed by clause and sequence number

## 4 Semantics of Electronic Attestation of Attributes
### 4.1 Introduction – Semantic areas
- **EAA metadata** (4.2), **Attested attributes** (4.3), **Attested attributes metadata** (4.4), **Key binding** (4.5), **Digital signature** (4.6)

### 4.2 EAA Metadata
#### 4.2.1 EAA specification
- **4.2.1.2 EAA type**  
  EAA-4.2.1.2-01: Shall indicate the type of the EAA.  
  EAA-4.2.1.2-02: Shall incorporate the EAA type.  
  EAA-4.2.1.2-03: Incorporation/value/placement depend on implementation.
- **4.2.1.3 EAA context**  
  EAA-4.2.1.3-01: If components have URLs as names, shall include context.  
  EAA-4.2.1.3-02: Context shall reference document mapping URLs to short forms.  
  EAA-4.2.1.3-03: Implementation‑dependent.
- **4.2.1.4 EAA schema**  
  EAA-4.2.1.4-01: Schema shall contain verification details for conformance.  
  EAA-4.2.1.4-02: May incorporate sequence of references.  
  EAA-4.2.1.4-03: Each reference shall include type identifier and URI.  
  EAA-4.2.1.4-04: Implementation‑dependent.

#### 4.2.2 EAA category
- **General**  
  EAA-4.2.2-01: Explicit signal identifying category.  
  EAA-4.2.2-02: May include.
- **QEAA**  
  QEAA-4.2.2.2-01: Shall include category, signalling QEAA.  
  QEAA-4.2.2.2-02: URI `urn:etsi:esi:eaa:eu:qualified`.
- **PuB-EAA**  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.2.3-01: Shall include explicit signal.  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.2.3-02: URI `urn:etsi:esi:eaa:eu:pub`.

#### 4.2.3 EAA identifier
- EAA-4.2.3-01: Shall unambiguously identify the EAA.  
- EAA-4.2.3-02: May incorporate.  
- EAA-4.2.3-03: Implementation‑dependent.

#### 4.2.4 EAA issuer identifier
- **General**  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-01: Shall unambiguously identify issuer.  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-02: May incorporate.  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-03: Implementation‑dependent.  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-04: May include EU Member State identifier (Alpha‑2 per ISO 3166‑1 [15]).  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-05: Value shall be Alpha‑2 country code.  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-06: For legal person, may include registration identifier.  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-07: May be built per EN 319 412‑1 clause 5.1.4.  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-08: May include legal person name.  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-09: If both VAT and other ID exist, VAT shall be used.  
  EAA-4.2.4.1-10: For natural person, may include name.
- **QEAA**  
  QEAA-4.2.4.2-01: Shall incorporate issuer identifier.  
  QEAA-4.2.4.2-02: Shall include Member State identifier.  
  QEAA-4.2.4.2-03: As per EAA-4.2.4.1-05.  
  QEAA-4.2.4.2-04: For legal person, shall include registration identifier (if exists).  
  QEAA-4.2.4.2-05: Built per EN 319 412‑1.  
  QEAA-4.2.4.2-06: Shall include name (legal person).  
  QEAA-4.2.4.2-07: Shall include name (natural person).  
  QEAA-4.2.4.2-08: Shall include download URL of signing certificate.
- **PuB-EAA**  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.4.3-01: Shall incorporate issuer identifier.  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.4.3-02: Shall include Member State identifier.  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.4.3-03: As per EAA-4.2.4.1-05.  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.4.3-04: Shall include registration identifier (if exists).  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.4.3-05: Built per EN 319 412‑1.  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.4.3-06: Shall include name of public body.  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.4.3-07: Shall include download URL of signing certificate.

#### 4.2.5 EAA issued on behalf
- EAA-4.2.5-01: May indicate if issued on behalf of another entity.  
- EAA-4.2.5-02: Implementation‑dependent.  
- EAA-4.2.5-03: May include identifier of entity on behalf.  
- EAA-4.2.5-04: May include name.  
- EAA-4.2.5-05: May include registration identifier (if legal person).  
- EAA-4.2.5-06: May be expressed per EAA-4.2.4.1-07.

#### 4.2.6 EAA subject and attribute subject identifiers and pseudonyms
- **4.2.6.2 EAA subject identifier**  
  EAA-4.2.6.2-01: May include identifier or pseudonym or none.  
  EAA-4.2.6.2-02: Implementation‑dependent.
- **4.2.6.3 EAA subject pseudonym**  
  EAA-4.2.6.3-01: Presence instead of identifier shall be clearly indicated.  
  EAA-4.2.6.3-02: Implementation‑dependent.
- **4.2.6.4 Attribute subject identifier**  
  EAA-4.2.6.4-01: May bind each attribute to identifier or pseudonym.  
  EAA-4.2.6.4-02: Implementation‑dependent.
- **4.2.6.5 Attribute subject pseudonym**  
  EAA-4.2.6.5-01: May incorporate pseudonyms.  
  EAA-4.2.6.5-02: Implementation‑dependent.  
  EAA-4.2.6.5-03: Binding mechanism implementation‑dependent.  
  EAA-4.2.6.5-04: Presence of pseudonym shall be clearly indicated.
- **4.2.6.6 Additional**  
  EAA-4.2.6.6-01: If multiple attribute subject identifiers, one shall be EAA subject identifier.  
  EAA-4.2.6.6-02: If only pseudonyms and no identifiers, one shall be EAA subject pseudonym.
- **QEAA**  
  QEAA-4.2.6.7-01: Shall include either identifier or pseudonym.  
  QEAA-4.2.6.7-02: All attributes shall refer to one entity (QEAA subject).
- **PuB-EAA**  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.6.8-01: Shall include either identifier or pseudonym.  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.6.8-02: All attributes shall refer to one entity.

#### 4.2.7 EAA issuance
- **General**  
  EAA-4.2.7.1-01: Issuance data shall identify date or date+time instant.  
  EAA-4.2.7.1-02: May incorporate.  
  EAA-4.2.7.1-03: Implementation‑dependent.
- **Time indication**  
  EAA-4.2.7.2-01: Instant time shall be UTC, include seconds, no fractions, no local offset.

#### 4.2.8 EAA validity periods
- **Common**  
  EAA-4.2.8.2-01: Validity period expressed by two UTC times.  
  EAA-4.2.8.2-02: Content per 4.2.7.2.  
  EAA-4.2.8.2-03: Implementation‑dependent.
- **Technical validity period**  
  EAA-4.2.8.3-01: Shall incorporate.
- **Administrative validity period**  
  EAA-4.2.8.4-01: May incorporate.

#### 4.2.9 Data constraining usage
- **4.2.9.2 EAA audience**  
  EAA-4.2.9.2-01: Identifies intended relying parties.  
  EAA-4.2.9.2-02: May incorporate.  
  EAA-4.2.9.2-03: Presence means intended only for those identified.  
  EAA-4.2.9.2-04: Value: sequence of RP identifiers, group identifiers, or combination.  
  EAA-4.2.9.2-05: Values depend on implementation.  
  EAA-4.2.9.2-06: Implementation‑dependent.
- **4.2.9.3 Signal of one-time use**  
  EAA-4.2.9.3-01: May incorporate.  
  EAA-4.2.9.3-02: Signal indicates one-time use, not retained.  
  EAA-4.2.9.3-03: Absence means no constraint.  
  EAA-4.2.9.3-04: Implementation‑dependent.

#### 4.2.10 Attributes evidence
- EAA-4.2.10-01: Contains evidence used by issuer.  
- EAA-4.2.10-02: May incorporate.  
- EAA-4.2.10-03: Sequence of one or more evidence.  
- EAA-4.2.10-04: Each evidence: include type identifier (URI), entity verifying, data items; may include identifier.  
- EAA-4.2.10-05: Implementation‑dependent.

#### 4.2.11 EAA status service
- **General**  
  EAA-4.2.11.1-01: Contains details of service for validity status.  
  EAA-4.2.11.1-02: May incorporate.  
  EAA-4.2.11.1-03: If short-lived (4.2.13), shall not incorporate.  
  EAA-4.2.11.1-04: Service shall include URI; may include type identifier.  
  EAA-4.2.11.1-05: Implementation‑dependent.
- **QEAA**  
  QEAA-4.2.11.2-01: If not short-lived, shall include status service.
- **PuB-EAA**  
  PuB-EAA-4.2.11.3-01: If not short-lived, shall include status service.

#### 4.2.12 EAA renewal service
- EAA-4.2.12-01: Contains details to request renewal.  
- EAA-4.2.12-02: May incorporate.  
- EAA-4.2.12-03: Shall include URI; may include type.  
- EAA-4.2.12-04: Implementation‑dependent.

#### 4.2.13 EAA short-lived
- EAA-4.2.13-01: May contain short-lived component.  
- EAA-4.2.13-02: Indicates validity too short to check revocation.

### 4.3 Attested attributes
- EAA-4.3-01: Each attribute shall have identifier unique in environment.  
- EAA-4.3-02: Each attribute shall have value (semantics depend on attribute).  
- EAA-4.3-03: Each attribute shall refer to one attribute subject.  
- EAA-4.3-04: May have format indicator for identifier.  
- EAA-4.3-05: May have type identifier.  
- EAA-4.3-06: May have friendly identifier.  
- EAA-4.3-07: May have component for individual reference.  
- EAA-4.3-08: Implementation‑dependent.

### 4.4 Attested attributes metadata
#### 4.4.2 Support to selective disclosure
- **4.4.2.2 Disclosure schema identifier**  
  EAA-4.4.2.2-01: May incorporate.  
  EAA-4.4.2.2-02: Shall univocally identify mechanism; may contain standard reference.  
  EAA-4.4.2.2-03: Implementation‑dependent.
- **4.4.2.3 Disclosure**  
  EAA-4.4.2.3-01: May have one or more disclosures.  
  EAA-4.4.2.3-02: Each disclosure shall contain attribute, binding data; may include version.  
  EAA-4.4.2.3-03: Implementation‑dependent.
- **4.4.2.4 Disclosure reference**  
  EAA-4.4.2.4-01: Each disclosure reference bound to one disclosure.  
  EAA-4.4.2.4-02: Binding ascertainable via algorithm.  
  EAA-4.4.2.4-03: May include validity info.  
  EAA-4.4.2.4-04: All disclosure references shall be signed by issuer.  
  EAA-4.4.2.4-05: Disclosures implies disclosure references; no disclosures implies no references.  
  EAA-4.4.2.4-06: Implementation‑dependent.
- **4.4.2.5 Disclosure algorithm identifier**  
  EAA-4.4.2.5-01: Identifies algorithm for binding.  
  EAA-4.4.2.5-02: May incorporate.  
  EAA-4.4.2.5-03: Shall contain algorithm identifier; may contain parameters.  
  EAA-4.4.2.5-04: Implementation‑dependent.

### 4.5 EAA data for key binding
- EAA-4.5-01: Should incorporate component proving public key possession by subject.  
- EAA-4.5-02: Value may be public key, certificate, or reference.  
- EAA-4.5-03: If pointer, shall contain digest of referenced object.  
- EAA-4.5-04: Recommended to be public key.

### 4.6 EAA digital signature
- **General**  
  EAA-4.6.1-01: Shall incorporate signature by issuer.  
  EAA-4.6.1-02: Where syntax allows, shall be AdES-B-B.  
  EAA-4.6.1-03: Selective disclosures placed in unsigned attributes container.  
  EAA-4.6.1-04: If AdES-B-B, disclosures in unsigned container per AdES spec.  
  EAA-4.6.1-05: May include signing certificate.  
  EAA-4.6.1-06: May include certification path (except trust anchor).  
  EAA-4.6.1-07: Signing certificate shall meet EAA profile per TS 119 412-6 [17].
- **QEAA**  
  QEAA-4.6.2-01: Signature shall be qualified electronic signature or seal.  
  QEAA-4.6.2-02: Shall contain signed attribute with reference to certificate download and digest.  
  QEAA-4.6.2-03: Should contain qualified certificate.  
  QEAA-4.6.2-04: Signing cert shall meet QEAA profile per TS 119 412-6 [17].
- **PuB-EAA**  
  PuB-EAA-4.6.3-01: Signature shall be qualified electronic signature or seal.  
  PuB-EAA-4.6.3-02: Shall contain signed attribute with reference and digest.  
  PuB-EAA-4.6.3-03: Should contain qualified certificate.  
  PuB-EAA-4.6.3-04: Signing cert shall meet PSB profile per TS 119 412-6 [17].

## 5 Implementation of EAA based on SD-JWT VC
- EAA-5.1-01: Shall be implemented as SD-JWT VC per [3].
- **EAA type**: `vct` claim with `vct#integrity` (EAA-5.2.1.2-01..03)
- **EAA context**: info in SD-JWT VC Type Metadata (no requirement)
- **EAA category**: non-QEAA/PuB-EAA shall not include `category` claim (EAA-5.2.2.1-01). QEAA: `category` = `urn:etsi:esi:eaa:eu:qualified`; PuB-EAA: `category` = `urn:etsi:esi:eaa:eu:pub`.
- **EAA identifier**: `jti` claim (EAA-5.2.3-01)
- **EAA issuer**: `issuing_authority` (optional, not if qualified certificate present), `issuing_country`, `iss_reg_id` (optional). QEAA & PuB-EAA: mandatory inclusion either in claims or in qualified certificate.
- **Subjects**: `sub` for identifier, `also_known_as` for pseudonym. QEAA/PuB-EAA: all attributes refer to subject.
- **Issuance**: `iat` claim.
- **Validity**: `nbf`, `exp` for technical; `adm_nbf`, `adm_exp` for administrative (both or none).
- **Usage constraints**: No audience component; `oneTime` claim (null type).
- **Attributes evidence**: `evidence` claim per [31].
- **Status service**: `status` JSON object with type, purpose, index, uri. QEAA/PuB-EAA: if not short-lived, mandatory.
- **Renewal**: Not implemented (EAA-5.2.11-01).
- **Short-lived**: `shortLived` (null type).
- **Attested attributes**: `subAttrs` for entity different than subject.
- **Selective disclosure**: `_sd` and `_sd_alg` per SD-JWT.
- **Key binding**: `cnf` claim (should).
- **Signature**: If not compact serialization, JAdES-B-B. QEAA/PuB-EAA: qualified signature, headers `x5u`, `x5t#S256`; should `x5c`.

## 6 Implementation of EAA based on ISO/IEC-mdoc
- Data structures per ISO/IEC 18013-5 [12] extended with namespace `org.etsi.01947201.010101`.
- If mDL: data elements in namespace `org.iso.18013.5.1`; else: `org.iso.23220.1`.
- **EAA type**: implicit by document type.
- **EAA context**: implicit via namespaces.
- **EAA category**: `category` data element (tstr). Non-qualified/PuB-EAA shall not include. QEAA: `urn:etsi:esi:eaa:eu:qualified`; PuB-EAA: `urn:etsi:esi:eaa:eu:pub`.
- **EAA identifier**: `document_number` (mandatory).
- **EAA issuer**: `issuing_authority` (mDL) or `issuing_authority_unicode` (non-mDL). `issuing_country` optional. `iss_reg_id` optional.
- **Subjects**: `given_name`, `family_name`, `document_number` for identifier; `also_known_as` for pseudonym. QEAA/PuB-EAA: all attributes refer to subject.
- **Issuance**: `issue_date` (mandatory).
- **Validity**: technical from `validFrom`/`validUntil` in MSO; administrative from `expiry_date` (mDL or generic).
- **Usage constraints**: No audience; `oneTime` (bool).
- **Attributes evidence**: Not implemented (EAA-6.2.9-01).
- **Status service**: `status` member in MSO; status_list per draft. QEAA/PuB-EAA: if not short-lived, mandatory.
- **Renewal**: Not implemented.
- **Short-lived**: `shortLived` (bool).
- **Attested attributes**: `IssuerSignedItem`; `SubAttr` for different subjects.
- **Selective disclosure**: `valueDigests` in MSO.
- **Key binding**: `deviceKey` in MSO (mandatory).
- **Signature**: CB-AdES. QEAA/PuB-EAA: qualified signature, headers `x5u`, `x5t` (SHA-256); should `x5chain`.

## 7 Implementation of EAA based on JSON-LD W3C-VC
- Uses JSON-LD serialization of W3C VC Data Model v2.0 [1].
- **EAA type**: `type` array (at least 2 strings).
- **EAA context**: `@context` array including `https://www.w3.org/ns/credentials/v2` and others.
- **EAA schema**: `credentialSchema` (optional) with `id` and `type` (JsonSchemaCredential, CddslSchemaCredential, ShaclSchemaCredential).
- **EAA category**: indicated in `type` array. QEAA: `urn:etsi:esi:eaa:eu:qualified`; PuB-EAA: `urn:etsi:esi:eaa:eu:pub`.
- **EAA identifier**: `id` property of credential.
- **EAA issuer**: `issuer` object with `id` (URI) and optional `issuing_authority` (string or multilingual object), `issuing_country`, `reg_id`. QEAA/PuB-EAA: mandatory inclusion of `issuing_authority` and `issuing_country`.
- **Issued on behalf**: `onBehalf` array in issuer.
- **Subjects**: `credentialSubject` array; each element has `id` or `pseudonym`. QEAA/PuB-EAA: exactly one element, with `id` or `pseudonym`.
- **Issuance**: `issuedAt` (optional).
- **Validity**: `validFrom`, `validUntil` (technical); `admValidFrom`, `admValidUntil` (administrative, optional).
- **Usage constraints**: No audience; `oneTime` (boolean); `termsOfUse` (optional) with type `embSDPolicy`.
- **Attributes evidence**: `evidence` array.
- **Status service**: `credentialStatus` with type (BitstringStatusListEntry, TokenStatusList, CRL, OCSP). If CRL/OCSP, `statusPurpose` = "revocation". QEAA/PuB-EAA: if not short-lived, mandatory.
- **Renewal**: `refreshService` (optional).
- **Short-lived**: `shortLived` (boolean, default false).
- **Attested attributes**: per `credentialSubject` array elements.
- **Selective disclosure**: Enveloping proofs use SD-JWT; embedded proofs use Data Integrity cryptosuites.
- **Key binding**: `cnf` property (should), same constraints as SD-JWT.
- **Signature**: Enveloping proofs (JOSE or SD-JWT) or embedded proofs. QEAA/PuB-EAA: qualified signature, headers `x5u`, `x5t#S256`; should `x5c`.

## 8 Implementation of EAA based on X.509 Attribute Certificates (X.509-AC)
- Based on IETF RFC 5755 [6]; ASN.1 definitions in separate file.
- **EAA type**: `etsi-eaaType` extension (optional) with URI string.
- **EAA context**: version v2; context derived from issuer/holder fields and extensions.
- **EAA schema**: not implemented.
- **EAA category**: `etsi-qeaaStatements` extension. QEAA: include `EUqeaaCompliance` statement; PuB-EAA: include `EUeaaPubACompliance` statement.
- **EAA identifier**: `serialNumber` (mandatory).
- **EAA issuer**: `issuer` field (Distinguished Name). For legal persons, no natural person attributes; may include `organizationIdentifier`. QEAA/PuB-EAA: must include country (C) and organizationName (O); must include `organizationIdentifier` if applicable; must include `authorityInfoAccess` with `id-ad-caIssuers`.
- **Subjects**: `holder` field with `objectDigestInfo` (public key cert digest). Pseudonym possible via privacy‑protecting cert.
- **Issuance**: No dedicated component.
- **Validity**: `notBeforeTime`/`notAfterTime` in `attrCertValidityPeriod` (technical). Administration: `etsi-eaaAdmValidityPeriod` extension.
- **Usage constraints**: Audience via `targetInformation` extension; one-time via `etsi-eaaOneTimeUse` extension.
- **Attributes evidence**: not implemented.
- **Status service**: via `authorityInfoAccess` (OCSP) or `cRLDistributionPoints`. If short-lived, include `noRevAvail` extension; otherwise one of the two.
- **Renewal**: not implemented.
- **Short-lived**: `etsi-eaaShortLived` extension; if present, no revocation info and must include `noRevAvail`.
- **Attested attributes**: `Attribute` instances in `attributes` field. Two sub‑implementations: JSON or ASN.1.
- **Selective disclosure**: via atomic EAAs (single attribute per AC) – recommended.
- **Key binding**: natively via `objectDigestInfo` pointing to subject public key certificate.
- **Signature**: QEAA/PuB-EAA requirements per 4.6.2/4.6.3 (qualified signature, certificate references).
