# ETSI TS 119 411-1: Electronic Signatures and Infrastructures (ESI); Policy and security requirements for Trust Service Providers issuing certificates; Part 1: General requirements
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V1.0.1 | **Date**: 2015-07 | **Type**: Normative
**Original**: http://www.etsi.org/standards-search

## Scope (Summary)
This Technical Specification defines generally applicable policy and security requirements for Trust Service Providers (TSPs) issuing public key certificates, including web site certificates. It supports six reference certificate policies (NCP, NCP+, LCP, EVCP, OVCP, DVCP) and provides a framework for defining additional policies. The document is applicable to certification supporting digital signatures, seals, TLS/SSL, and authentication/encryption, but does not specify assessment methods.

## Definitions and Abbreviations
- **Certificate**: Public key of a user, rendered un-forgeable by encipherment with the private key of the CA (per X.509).
- **Certificate Policy (CP)**: Named set of rules indicating applicability of a certificate to a particular community.
- **Certificate Revocation List (CRL)**: Signed list of certificates no longer considered valid.
- **Certification Authority (CA)**: Authority trusted to create and assign certificates.
- **Certification Practice Statement (CPS)**: Statement of practices a CA employs in issuing, managing, revoking, renewing, or re-keying certificates.
- **Extended Validation Certificate (EVC)**: As defined in EVCG [4].
- **Publicly-trusted certificate (PTC)**: Certificate trusted by virtue of its Root Certificate being distributed as a trust anchor in widely-available software.
- **Registration Authority (RA)**: Entity responsible for identification and authentication of subjects.
- **Secure cryptographic device**: Device that holds the user's private key, protects it against compromise, and performs signing or decryption functions.
- **Abbreviations**: CA, CAB Forum, CP, CPS, CRL, CSP, DVC, DVCP, EAL, EVC, EVCG, EVCP, LCP, NCP, NCP+, OCSP, OVC, OVCP, PDS, PKI, PTC, RA, TLS/SSL, TSP, UTC.
- **Notation**: Requirements are marked by the applicable CP: [LCP], [NCP], [NCP+], [EVCP], [OVCP], [DVCP]; [CONDITIONAL] for conditional requirements; [PTC] for CAB Forum requirements.

## 4 General Concepts
### 4.1 General policy requirements concepts
See ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 4 and IETF RFC 3647 [i.3] clauses 3.1 and 3.4.

### 4.2 Certificate policy and certification practice statement
- **4.2.1 Overview**: The TSP develops CPS and CP. CP states "what is to be adhered to"; CPS states "how it is adhered to".
- **4.2.2 Purpose**: CP referenced by a policy identifier in a certificate.
- **4.2.3 Level of specificity**: CP is higher level; CPS defines how one CA meets CP requirements.
- **4.2.4 Approach**: CP independent of operating environment; CPS tailored to CA.
- **4.2.5 Certificate Policy**: Six CPs defined:
  - **NCP**: Normalized Certificate Policy for general transactions.
  - **NCP+**: NCP requiring a secure cryptographic device.
  - **LCP**: Lightweight Certificate Policy (less onerous).
  - **EVCP**: Extended Validation Certificate Policy (for TLS/SSL, built on NCP + EVCG [4]).
  - **DVCP**: Domain Validation Certificate Policy (built on LCP + BRG [5]).
  - **OVCP**: Organizational Validation Certificate Policy (built on LCP + BRG [5]).
- Clause 7 specifies a framework for other CPs.

### 4.3 Other Trust Service Providers statements
TSP issues terms and conditions; PKI disclosure statement is part of those.

### 4.4 Certification services
Component services: Registration, Certificate generation, Dissemination, Revocation management, Revocation status, and optionally Subject device provision.

## 5 General provisions on Certification Practice Statement and Certificate Policies
### 5.1 General requirements
- The document is structured in line with IETF RFC 3647 [i.3].
- Requirements are expressed as security objectives and specific controls.
- **[NCP+, EVCP]**: All requirements for [NCP] shall apply.
- **[DVCP, OVCP]**: All requirements for [LCP] shall apply.

### 5.2 Certification Practice Statement requirements
- General requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 6.1 shall apply.
- a) The TSP CPS **should** be structured per IETF RFC 3647 [i.3].
- b) The CPS **shall** include the complete CA hierarchy.
- c) The CPS **shall** include signature algorithms and parameters employed.
- d) The TSP **shall** publicly disclose its CPS online 24x7.
- e) [PTC]: Clause 2.2 of BRG [5] and clause 8.3 of EVCG [4] **shall** apply.
- f) [PTC]: Clause 2 of BRG [5] and clause 8.2.1 of EVCG [4] **shall** apply.
- g) [CONDITIONAL]: When CP document issued separately, it **shall** include all rules for the given CP.
- h) The CPS **shall** specify practice regarding use of CA keys for signing certificates, CRLs, and OCSP.

### 5.3 Certificate Policy name and identification
- Policy identifiers (OIDs) defined for each CP:
  - **NCP**: `itu-t(0) identified-organization(4) etsi(0) other-certificate-policies(2042) policy-identifiers(1) ncp (1)`
  - **NCP+**: `... ncpplus (2)`
  - **LCP**: `... lcp (3)`
  - **EVCP**: `... evcp (4)`
  - **DVCP**: `... dvcp (6)`
  - **OVCP**: `... ovcp (7)`
- If changes affect applicability, the policy identifier **should** be changed.

### 5.4 PKI participants
#### 5.4.1 Certification Authority
- CA has overall responsibility for certification services; may subcontract but maintains responsibility.
- CA is a type of TSP.
- TSP may include a hierarchy of CAs; TSP ensures subordinate CAs comply with applicable policies.

#### 5.4.2 Subscriber and subject
- Subject can be: natural person, natural person associated with legal person, legal person, or device/system.
- Subscriber may act on behalf of subject; responsibilities addressed in clause 6.3.4.
- To avoid conflicts of interest, Subscriber and RA organization are separate entities except when the organization runs RA for itself.

#### 5.4.3 Others
- Other participants may be identified by the TSP.

### 5.5 Certificate usage
- NCP, NCP+, LCP place no constraints on user community.
- EV certificates purpose per EVCG [4] clause 2.
- PTC purpose per BRG [5] clause 1.4.1.
- Certificates under EVCG [4] or BRG [5] are for publicly trusted web server certificates.

## 6 Trust Service Providers practice
### 6.1 Publication and repository responsibilities
- TSP **shall** make certificates available to subscribers, subjects, relying parties.
- a) Upon generation, certificate **shall** be available to subscriber/subject.
- b) Certificates **shall** be available for retrieval only with subject's consent.
- c) TSP **shall** make terms and conditions available to relying parties (see 6.8.10).
- d) Terms **shall** be readily identifiable for a given certificate.
- e) [CONDITIONAL]:
  - [LCP]: information in b) and c) **shall** be available as specified in CPS.
  - [NCP]: information **shall** be available 24/7; best endeavours to minimise unavailability.
- f) [CONDITIONAL]: If issuing to public, information in c) **shall** be publicly and internationally available.
- g) Information in c) **should** be publicly and internationally available.

### 6.2 Identification and authentication
#### 6.2.1 Naming
- Requirements per X.509 [6], RFC 5280 [7], and appropriate part of ETSI TS 119 412 [2], [9], [10].

#### 6.2.2 Initial identity validation
- TSP **shall** verify identity of subscriber and subject; check requests are accurate, authorized, complete.
- a) TSP **shall** collect direct evidence or attestation of identity (name, attributes) and any specific attributes. [PTC]: verification methods per EVCG [4] clause 11 and BRG [5] clause 3.2.
- b) [CONDITIONAL] [NCP]: Natural person identity shall be checked by physical presence or equivalent assurance.
- c) [CONDITIONAL]: Evidence of full name or date/place of birth, etc.
- d) [CONDITIONAL] [NCP]: Natural person associated with legal person: similar to b).
- e) [CONDITIONAL]: Evidence of full name, date/place of birth, legal person name and registration, affiliation.
- f) [CONDITIONAL] [NCP]: Legal person: identity checked by physical presence of mandated representative or equivalent.
- g) [CONDITIONAL]: Legal person: evidence of full name, national identification. [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 3.2.2; [EVCP]: EVCG [4] clause 11.2.
- h) [CONDITIONAL] [NCP]: Device/system operated by legal person: similar to f).
- i) [CONDITIONAL]: Device identifier, organizational name, registration info. [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 3.2.2; [EVCP]: EVCG [4] clause 11.2.1.
- j) [CONDITIONAL] [NCP]: Device/system operated by natural person: identity checked by physical presence or equivalent.
- k) [CONDITIONAL]: Device identifier, nationally recognized identity number. [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 3.2.3.
- l) TSP **shall** record all info necessary to verify identity, including reference numbers.
- m) [CONDITIONAL]: If subscriber not subject, evidence of authorization shall be provided.
- n) Subscriber **shall** provide physical address or contact attributes.
- o) TSP **shall** provide evidence of compliance with data protection legislation.
- p) TSP's verification policy **shall** only capture evidence sufficient for intended use.

#### 6.2.3 Identification and authentication for Re-key requests
- Requests must be complete, accurate, authorized.
- a) CA **shall** issue new certificate using previously certified public key only if cryptographic security still sufficient and no sign of compromise or revocation.

#### 6.2.4 Identification and authentication for revocation requests
- TSP **shall** revoke certificates in a timely manner based on authorized requests.
- a) TSP **shall** document in CPS revocation procedures including:
  - i) Who can submit reports/requests.
  - ii) How submitted.
  - iii) Confirmation requirements.
  - iv) Reasons for suspension/revocation. [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 4.9.
  - v) Distribution mechanism.
  - vi) Maximum delay between receipt and decision to change status: at most 24 hours.
  - vii) Maximum delay between confirmation and actual change of status available to relying parties: at most 60 minutes.
  - viii) Time for revocation services **shall** be synchronized with UTC at least once a day.
- b) Requests/reports **shall** be processed on receipt.
- c) Requests/reports **shall** be authenticated and from authorized source.

### 6.3 Certificate Life-Cycle operational requirements
#### 6.3.1 Certificate application
- a) [CONDITIONAL]: If subject's key pair not generated by CA, certificate request process **shall** check that subject has possession of private key.
- b) [EVCP]: Dual control procedure per EVCG [4] clause 14.1.3.

#### 6.3.2 Certificate application processing
- Applications shall be from trusted registration service.
- a) [CONDITIONAL]: When external RAs used, registration data exchanged only with recognized and authenticated providers.

#### 6.3.3 Certificate issuance
- CA **shall** issue certificates securely to maintain authenticity.
- a) Certificate profiles per clause 6.6.1.
- b) CA **shall** take measures against forgery; guarantee confidentiality if CA generates subject keys.
- c) Procedure of issuing certificate **shall** be securely linked to registration, renewal, or rekey.
- d) [CONDITIONAL]: If CA generated subject key:
  - i) procedure securely linked to key generation;
  - ii) [LCP] and [NCP] private key securely passed to registered subject or TSP managing it;
  - iii) [NCP+] secure cryptographic device securely delivered.
- e) Over lifetime of CA, a distinguished name used in a certificate **shall** never be reassigned.
- f) Root CA keys **shall** be under at least dual control. [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 4.3.
- g) Certificate issuance by root CA **shall** be under control of authorized personnel. [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 4.3.
- h) [CONDITIONAL]: If certificate issued to natural person subscribed by legal person, organizational identifier in certificate represents subscriber, subject identifier is natural person.
- i) Use of policy identifier: For each CP, identifier shall be the OID specified in clause 5.3 item for that CP, and/or an OID allocated by TSP or further standardisation.

#### 6.3.4 Certificate acceptance
- Terms and conditions shall be available and indicate acceptance.
- a) Before entering into contract, TSP **shall** inform subscriber of terms (clause 6.9.4).
- b) [CONDITIONAL]: If subject is a person different from subscriber, subject informed of obligations.
- c) i) Terms communicated through durable means; ii) may be electronic; iii) may use model PKI disclosure statement (annex A).
- d) TSP **shall** record signed agreement with subscriber.
- e) [CONDITIONAL]: Separate subscriber and subject (natural person): agreement in two parts with specified content.
- f) [CONDITIONAL]: Same entity: agreement may be in one or two parts.
- g) Agreement may be in electronic form.
- h) Records retained for period indicated to subscriber.

#### 6.3.5 Key pair and certificate usage
- Subscriber obligations shall include: a) accurate information; b) key use per limitations; c) avoid unauthorized use; d) [CONDITIONAL] if subscriber/subject generates keys: use recognized algorithm and key length; e) [CONDITIONAL] if subject generates signing keys: maintain sole control; f) [NCP+] only use private key in secure cryptographic device; g) [NCP+] generate keys within secure cryptographic device; h) notify TSP without reasonable delay of loss, compromise, or inaccuracy; i) immediately discontinue use after compromise; j) cease use if CA compromised.
- Relying party obligations: k) verify validity using current status; l) consider usage limitations; m) take other prescribed precautions.

#### 6.3.6 Certificate renewal
- Requests must be complete, accurate, authorized.
- a) i) TSP **shall** check existence and validity of certificate to be renewed and that identity info still valid.
  - ii) [EVCP]: EVCG [4] clauses 9.4 and 11.3.
  - iii) [OVCP] and [DVCP]: BRG [5] clauses 6.3.2 and 3.3.1.
- b) If terms changed, communicate and agree per clause 6.3.4.
- c) Requirements h) to l) of clause 6.2.2 apply.

#### 6.3.7 Certificate Re-key
- See clause 6.2.3.

#### 6.3.8 Certificate modification
- Requests must be complete, accurate, authorized.
- a) If names/attributes changed or previous certificate revoked, registration info verified and agreed per clause 6.2.2 items a) to j).

#### 6.3.9 Certificate revocation and suspension
- TSP **shall** revoke certificates in a timely manner.
- a) Subject/subscriber informed of change of status.
- b) Once definitively revoked, certificate **shall not** be reinstated.
- c) [CONDITIONAL]: If CRLs used: published at least daily; each CRL states next issue time; may publish earlier; signed by CA or designated entity. [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 4.10.2.
- d) [OVCP] and [DVCP]: TSP **shall** operate and maintain certificate status information; BRG [5] clause 4.10.2.
- e) [EVCP]: TSP **shall** comply with EVCG [4] clause 13.
- f) [CONDITIONAL]: If CARL used, new CARL at least once a year with nextUpdate at most 1 year.

#### 6.3.10 Certificate status services
- TSP **shall** provide status checking services.
- a) Status info available 24/7; best endeavours to minimise unavailability.
- b) Integrity and authenticity **shall** be protected.
- c) Status info includes at least until certificate expires.
- d) OCSP **shall** be supported.
- e) CRL **may** be supported.
- f) [CONDITIONAL]: If multiple methods, updates consistent across all.
- g) Status info **shall** be publicly and internationally available.

#### 6.3.11 End of subscription
- No policy requirement.

#### 6.3.12 Key escrow and recovery
- a) [CONDITIONAL]: If key used for digital signatures, CA **shall not** hold copies of subject's private signing keys in a way that results in use not under sole control of signatory. Security of duplicates at same level; number minimized.
- b) [CONDITIONAL]: If CA keeps copy, CA **shall** keep secret and only make available to authorized persons per a).

### 6.4 Facility, management, and operational controls
#### 6.4.1 General
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clauses 5, 6.3, 7.3 **shall** apply.

#### 6.4.2 Physical security controls
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 7.6 **shall** apply. Additional:
- a) Facilities for certificate generation and revocation management **shall** be physically protected.
- b) Every entry to physically secure area subject to independent oversight; non-authorized persons accompanied; every entry/exit logged.
- c) Clear security perimeters around certificate generation and revocation management.
- d) Physical and environmental security controls for facility, system resources, supporting facilities.
- e) Controls against unauthorized off-site removal.
- f) Other functions may be in same secured area if access limited.
- g) Root CA keys held and used physically isolated from normal operations; only designated trusted personnel access for signing subordinate CA certificates.

#### 6.4.3 Procedural controls
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 7.4 items b), c), d), e) **shall** apply.
- a) Multi-factor authentication for accounts capable of directly causing certificate issuance.
- b) Dissemination application **shall** enforce access control on adds/deletes/modifications.
- c) Revocation status application **shall** enforce access control on modifications.

#### 6.4.4 Personnel controls
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 7.2 **shall** apply.
- a) In addition to trusted roles in [8] 7.2 g), registration and revocation officers responsibilities (per CEN TS 419 261 [i.9]) **shall** be supported.
- b) [PTC]: role of validation specialist **shall** be included per BRG [5] and EVCG [4].

#### 6.4.5 Audit logging procedures
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 7.10 **shall** apply.
- a) All security events logged (profile changes, start-up/shutdown, crashes, hardware failures, firewall/router activities, PKI access attempts).
- b) All registration events logged.
- c) Registration information recorded: type of documents, unique ID, storage location, subscriber agreement choices, identity of accepting entity, validation method, name of TSP/RA.
- d) TSP **shall** maintain privacy of subject information.
- e, f, g) Log all events relating to life-cycle of CA keys, certificates, keys managed by CA.
- h) Log all revocation requests/reports and actions.

#### 6.4.6 Records archival
- a) TSP **shall** retain for at least seven years after any certificate ceases to be valid:
  - i) log of all events relating to life-cycle of keys managed by CA;
  - ii) documentation as identified in clause 6.3.4.

#### 6.4.7 Key changeover
- No policy requirement.

#### 6.4.8 Compromise and disaster recovery
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clauses 7.9 and 7.11 **shall** apply.
- a) Backup systems data necessary to resume CA operations.
- b) In line with ISO/IEC 27002 [i.7] clause 12.3: regular backups, adequate facilities, regular testing.
- c) Backup/restore by relevant trusted roles.
- d) [CONDITIONAL]: Dual control for recovery if risk analysis indicates.
- e) Business continuity plan shall address compromise of CA signing key.
- f) After disaster, take steps to avoid repetition.
- g) In case of compromise, TSP **shall** as minimum: inform subscribers and entities, indicate certificates may no longer be valid, revoke any CA certificate for compromised TSP.
- h) If algorithms become insufficient, TSP **shall** inform subscribers and relying parties, revoke affected certificates.

#### 6.4.9 Certification Authority or Registration Authority termination
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 7.12 **shall** apply.
- a) Apply to registration info, revocation status info, event logs for their respective periods.
- b) Include handling of revocation status for unexpired certificates.

### 6.5 Technical security controls
#### 6.5.1 Key pair generation and installation
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 7.5 **shall** apply.
- a) CA key generation in physically secured environment, by trusted roles under at least dual control; number of personnel minimized.
- b) CA key generation using recognized algorithm for CA signing.
- c) Selected key length and algorithm recognized by industry.
- d) Before expiration of CA signing key, generate new key pair and take actions to avoid disruption.
- e) Suitable interval between expiry and last signed certificate.
- f) Documented procedure for key generation on root and subordinate CAs, including roles, functions, responsibilities, evidence collection.
- g) Report signed:
  - i) For root CA: by trusted role and independent witness (e.g. notary, auditor).
  - ii) For subordinate CAs: by trusted role.
  - iii) [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 6.1.1.1.
- h) CA signature verification keys available to relying parties with integrity assurance.
- [CONDITIONAL] If CA generates subject keys:
  - i) Use recognized algorithm.
  - j) Appropriate key length and algorithm.
  - k) Generate and store securely.
- l) Subject private key delivered securely; if compromised, revoke all relevant certificates. [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 6.1.24.
- m) Copies of subject private key held by TSP destroyed after delivery.
- n) [NCP+]: TSP **shall** secure issuance of secure cryptographic device; preparation, storage, distribution secure.

#### 6.5.2 Private key protection and cryptographic module engineering controls
- In addition to 6.5.1:
- a) CA key generation in secure cryptographic device meeting ISO/IEC 19790 [3] or equivalent (e.g. FIPS 140-2 level 3) OR trustworthy system assured EAL 4+ per ISO/IEC 15408 [1] to suitable protection profile.
- b) CA private signing key held and used in secure cryptographic device (same standards).
- c) [CONDITIONAL]: When outside device, same level of protection.
- d) CA private signing key backed up, stored, recovered only by trusted roles under dual control in physically secured environment; number minimized.
- e) Backup copies subject to same or greater security.
- f) [CONDITIONAL]: If stored in dedicated device, access controls ensure keys not accessible outside.
- g, h, i) Secure cryptographic device not tampered during shipment, storage; functioning correctly.
- j) CA private signing keys destroyed upon device retirement.

#### 6.5.3 Other aspects of key pair management
- a) CA signing keys used for certificates and/or revocation status **shall not** be used for any other purpose.
- b) Certificate signing keys used only within physically secure premises.
- c) Use compatible with hash algorithm, signature algorithm, key length per current practice.
- d) All copies of CA private signing keys destroyed or put beyond use at end of life cycle.
- e) [CONDITIONAL]: Self-signed certificate attributes compliant with X.509 key usage and aligned with c).

#### 6.5.4 Activation data
- a) Installation, activation, recovery of CA signing keys in secure device requires simultaneous control of at least two trusted employees.
- [CONDITIONAL] If TSP issues secure cryptographic device:
  - b) Deactivation and reactivation done securely.
  - c) Activation data (e.g. PIN) prepared and distributed separately from device.

#### 6.5.5 Computer security controls
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 7.4 items a) and f) **shall** apply.
- a) Local network components in physically and logically secure environment; configurations periodically checked.
- b) Multi-factor authentication for accounts capable of directly causing certificate issuance.
- c) Dissemination application enforce access control.
- d) Revocation status application enforce access control.
- e) Continuous monitoring and alarm facilities to detect unauthorized/irregular access attempts.

#### 6.5.6 Life cycle security controls
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 7.7 **shall** apply.
- a) [NCP]: Capacity demands monitored and projections made.
- b) [PTC]: BRG [5] clause 5.
- c) See 6.5.5 e).

#### 6.5.7 Network security controls
- Requirements from ETSI TS 119 401 [8] clause 7.8 **shall** apply.
- a) TSP **shall** maintain and protect CA systems in at least a secure zone; implement security procedure for communications between secure zones and high security zones.
- b) TSP **shall** configure CA systems by removing/disabling unused accounts, applications, services, protocols, ports.
- c) TSP **shall** grant access to secure zones and high security zones only to trusted roles.
- d) Root CA system **shall** be in a high security zone.

#### 6.5.8 Timestamping
- Not in scope. See ETSI TS 119 421 [i.16].

#### 6.6.3 OCSP profile
- OCSP as defined in IETF RFC 6960 [11].

## 7 Framework for the definition of other certificate policies
### 7.1 Certificate policy management
- Authority issuing CP other than those in clause 5 shall demonstrate CP is effective.
- a) CP **shall** identify which defined CP it adopts as basis plus variances.
- b) There **shall** be a body (e.g. policy management authority) with final authority for specifying and approving CP.
- c) Risk assessment **should** be carried out.
- d) CPs **should** be approved and modified per defined review process.
- e) Defined review process to ensure CP supported by CPS.
- f) TSP **should** make CPs available to user community.
- g) Revisions to CPs **should** be made available to subscribers and relying parties.
- h) CP **shall** incorporate or further constrain all requirements in clauses 5 and 6 where without specific marking.
- i) CP **shall** specify X.509 certificate profile requirements.
- j) [CONDITIONAL]: ETSI TS 119 412 parts 2-4 **should** be used where appropriate.
- k) Unique OID obtained for CP.

### 7.2 Additional requirements
- Subscribers and relying parties **shall** be informed, as part of clause 6.9.4, of how the specific policy adds to or constrains the CP.
