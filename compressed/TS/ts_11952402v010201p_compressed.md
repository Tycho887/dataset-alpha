# ETSI TS 119 524-2 V1.2.1: Electronic Signatures and Infrastructures (ESI); Testing Conformance and Interoperability of Electronic Registered Delivery Services; Part 2: Test suites for interoperability testing of Electronic Registered Delivery Service Providers
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V1.2.1 | **Date**: 2023-12 | **Type**: Technical Specification (Normative)
**Original**: https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119500_119599/11952402/01.02.01_60/ts_11952402v010201p.pdf

## Scope (Summary)
Defines a test suite for interoperability testing of Electronic Registered Delivery Services (ERDS) as specified in ETSI EN 319 522 series. Covers three deployment models: black-box (same ERDS for sender and recipients), 4-corner (different ERDS without intermediate), and extended (different ERDS with intermediate IERDS). Also provides a mechanism for documenting and expanding the test suite.

## 4 Technical Approach

### 4.1 Components of Test Cases and Identifiers
- Test cases defined via parameterized scenarios (Clause 5), ERD message instances (Clause 6), and named sets (Clause 7).
- Functional notation: `TestCase#i = Scenario_id(ReceivingSide, ERD message 1 details, ..., ERD message N details, AdditionalRequirementsSetId)`
- ERD message instances composed of metadata combinations (AS4 profiled + relay metadata) and payloads (user content, ERDS evidence).
- Metadata combinations defined in Clauses 6.2.2 (AS4) and 6.2.3 (relay metadata) are assigned unique identifiers.

### 4.2 Adding New Test Cases
- Steps: identify receiving entities, define ERDSs, define scenario (if new), assign SCN_ID, define ERD messages (check/reuse metadata combinations), identify evidence sets, define additional requirements.

## 5 Scenarios

### 5.1 Introduction
Scenarios cover three models (black-box, 4-corner, extended) based on ETSI EN 319 522-1. Template (Table 1) includes: Scenario id, Purpose, Parameter list (ERD messages, evidence sets), Sequence of actions (steps per actor: Sender, ERDS(s), Receiving side).

### 5.2 Abbreviations Used in Scenario Tables
- ERDS evidence abbreviations as per Table 2 (e.g., XML_SUB_ACC, XML_REL_REJ, etc.). XML prefix indicates XML format. PDF alternatives are possible but not detailed.
- SERDS, RERDS, IERDS denote sender, recipient, and intermediate ERDS.

### 5.3 Black-Box Model Scenarios
#### 5.3.1 Introduction
Sender and recipients subscribed to same ERDS. Sub-clauses: without notification for acceptance (5.3.2) and with notification for acceptance (5.3.3).

#### 5.3.2 Scenarios Without Notification for Acceptance (Tables 3 – 3i)
10 scenarios (ERDS_BB_NO_NOT_F_ACC#1 to #10):
- #1: Negative – submission rejected, ERDS returns {hndvMet, XML_SUB_REJ}.
- #2: Positive – submission accepted, consignment of {hndvMet, user content} to N recipients, ContentConsignment evidence.
- #3: Positive – adds retrieval of ERD dispatch and ContentHandover evidence.
- #4: Negative – one retrieval fails, generates ContentHandoverFailure in addition to ContentHandover.
- #5: Positive – consignment includes SubmissionAcceptance evidence in payload.
- #6: Positive – adds notification of consignment and ConsignmentNotification evidence.
- #7: Negative – one notification fails, generates ConsignmentNotificationFailure.
- #8: Negative – one consignment fails, generates ContentConsignmentFailure.
- #9: Positive – uses ContentAccessTracking instead of ContentHandover.
- #10: Positive – consignment includes SubmissionAcceptance evidence and handover.

#### 5.3.3 Scenarios With Notification for Acceptance (Tables 4 – 4f)
7 scenarios (ERDS_BB_NOT_F_ACC#1 to #7):
- Adds NotificationForAcceptance to receiving side before consignment.
- #1: Positive – all respond positively, logs NotificationForAcceptance, NotificationDelivered, ConsignmentAcceptance, ContentConsignment.
- #2: Negative – one recipient rejects consignment, generates ConsignmentRejection.
- #3: Negative – one recipient does not answer in time, generates AcceptanceRejectionExpiry.
- #4: Positive – adds ContentHandover after retrieval.
- #5: Negative – one handover fails, generates ContentHandoverFailure.
- #6: Negative – combination of rejection and handover failure.
- #7: Positive – uses ContentAccessTracking instead of ContentHandover.

### 5.4 Scenarios for 4-Corner Model
#### 5.4.1 Introduction
Sender and recipients subscribed to different ERDS (SERDS and RERDS) without intermediate IERDS. Sub-clauses: RERDS does not use notification for acceptance (5.4.2) and uses (5.4.3).

#### 5.4.2 Scenarios Where RERDS Does Not Use Notification for Acceptance (Tables 5 – 5f)
7 scenarios (4C_RERDS_NO_NOT_F_ACC#1 to #7):
- #1: Negative – RERDS rejects relay, returns {hndvMet, EV_SET#1} containing XML_REL_REJ and XML_SUB_ACC.
- #2: Positive – relay accepted, consignment succeeds, receipts carry XML_REL_ACC and XML_CONT_CONS separately.
- #3: Positive – receipts combine XML_REL_ACC and XML_CONT_CONS, after retrieval sends XML_CONT_HAND.
- #4: Negative – one handover fails, receipt includes XML_CONT_HAND and XML_CONT_HAND_FAIL.
- #5: Negative – one consignment fails, receipt includes XML_CONT_CONS and XML_CONT_CONS_FAIL.
- #6: Variation of #5 using ERD payload instead of dispatch.
- #7: Negative – uses ContentAccessTracking and ContentHandoverFailure.

#### 5.4.3 Scenarios Where RERDS Uses Notification for Acceptance (Tables 6 – 6e)
6 scenarios (4C_RERDS_NOT_F_ACC#1 to #6):
- #1: Positive – full cycle: relay, notification, consignment, handover, all evidence sent in one receipt.
- #2: Negative – one recipient rejects notification, yields XML_CONS_REJ.
- #3: Negative – one recipient does not answer in time, yields XML_ACC_REJ_EXP.
- #4: Negative – one consignment fails, yields XML_CONT_CONS_FAIL.
- #5: Negative – one handover fails, yields XML_CONT_HAND_FAIL.
- #6: Negative – uses ContentAccessTracking and ContentConsignmentFailure.

### 5.5 Scenarios for Extended Model
#### 5.5.1 Introduction
Includes one IERDS between SERDS and RERDS. Sub-clauses similar to 4-corner.

#### 5.5.2 Scenarios Where RERDS Does Not Use Notification for Acceptance (Tables 7 – 7e)
6 scenarios (EXT_RERDS_NO_NOT_F_ACC#1 to #6):
- #1: Positive – relay through IERDS, consignment, handover, receipts propagate back.
- #2: Negative – consignment fails at RERDS, receipts with XML_CONT_CONS and XML_CONT_CONS_FAIL.
- #3: Negative – handover fails, receipts with XML_CONT_HAND and XML_CONT_HAND_FAIL.
- #4: Negative – IERDS rejects relay, returns XML_REL_REJ.
- #5: Negative – RERDS rejects relay after IERDS acceptance, returns XML_REL_REJ.
- #6: Positive – uses ContentAccessTracking.

#### 5.5.3 Scenarios Where RERDS Uses Notification for Acceptance (Tables 8 – 8b)
3 scenarios (SERDS_SN_IERDS_SF_RERDS_BB_NO_NOT_F_ACC#1 to #3):
- #1: Positive – relay, notification, consignment, receipts with evidence sets.
- #2: Negative – one recipient rejects notification, yields ConsignmentRejection.
- #3: Negative – one recipient rejects notification and later handover fails, complex evidence sets.

## 6 ERD Messages Instances

### 6.1 Introduction and Technical Approach
Defines instances of ERD notifications, payloads, receipts, and dispatches using metadata combinations from clauses 6.2.2 (AS4 profiled) and 6.2.3 (relay metadata). Each instance is a composition of these combos.

### 6.2 Combinations of Fields for Headers in ERD Envelopes
#### 6.2.1 Introduction
Clause 6.2.2 defines AS4 metadata combinations; 6.2.3 defines relay metadata combinations; 6.2.4 defines aggregations.

#### 6.2.2 Combinations of AS4 Metadata Profiled in ETSI EN 319 522-4 (Table 9)
- Only combination defined: `AS4Met#1`. Contains mandatory/recommended values from ETSI EN 319 522-4-1 for PMode fields (Initiator, Responder, Role, Service, Security, Duplication elimination). Notes: 1 – signed receipts; 2 – signed receipts and error signal messages.

#### 6.2.3 Combinations of Components of Relay Metadata (Table 10)
8 parameterized combinations (RelayMet#1 to #8) with fields:
- `RelayTime`, `ExpirationTime`, `ScheduledDeliveryTime`, `ApplicablePolicy` (all AS_PER_TESTCASE).
- `RequiredAssuranceLevel` (Parameter) present in RelayMet#2, #3, #4, #5, and their variants without ScheduledDelivery (#7, #8).
- `RequestedConsignmentMode` (Parameter) present in RelayMet#3, #5, #8.
- Additional requirements (a–d) enforce temporal ordering: (a) RelayTime earlier than Expiration and ScheduledDelivery; (b) Expiration later than RelayTime and ScheduledDelivery; (c) ScheduledDelivery between RelayTime and Expiration; (d) ScheduledDelivery later than RelayTime and Expiration (for negative tests).
- RelayMet#1–#5 include ScheduledDeliveryTime; #6–#8 omit it.

#### 6.2.4 Combinations of AS4 Metadata Profiled and Relay Metadata (Table 11)
- Metadata instance = `Metadata(AS4Met#1, RelayMet#I, AssLevelComb, ConsignmentModeId)`.
- AssLevelComb: nil, low/low, substantial/substantial, high/high.
- ConsignmentModeId: nil, basic, consented, consentedSigned.
- Table 11 lists 27 combos for cases with and without ScheduledDelivery, plus 2 negative combos (RelayMet#4 and #5 with low/low).

### 6.3 Instances of ERD Payload
- Defined as: `ERD_PayloadInst(Metadata(...), UserContent)`.
- As many instances as metadata combos in 6.2.4.

### 6.4 Instances of ERDS Receipts
- Defined as: `ERD_ReceiptInst(Metadata(AS4Met#1, RelayMet#J, nil, nil), <EVID#M>+)` where J from 1 to 9 and evidence set M includes as many evidence payloads as specified in the test case.

### 6.5 Instances of ERD Dispatch
- Defined as: `ERDS_DispatchInst(Metadata(AS4Met#1, RelayMet#I, AssLevelComb, ConsignmentModeId), <EVID#M>+)` with parameters as in 6.4.

## 7 Other Named Sets

### 7.1 Named Sets of Participating ERDSs
- Pattern: `ERDS(I,J)` where I = number of IERDS, J = number of RERDS.

### 7.2 Named Sets of Additional Requirements (Table 12)
- `AdditionalReqs#1`: Original message not signed, no attachment, sent by sender.
- `AdditionalReqs#2`: Original message not signed, no attachment, sent by sender's delegate.

### 7.3 Named Sets of Entities in Receiving Side
- Pattern: `RecSide(I,J,K)` where I = number of recipients, J = number of recipient's delegates, K = number of recipients each delegate is delegate of (K ≤ I, if I≠0 then K≠0).

## 8 Test Cases Definition

### 8.1 Introduction
#### 8.1.1 General
Test cases are sets expressed as functions of parameters (integers, tuples, enumerated failure reasons). Specific test case is obtained by assigning values to all parameters.

#### 8.1.2 Notation for Black Box Model Scenarios
- Notation: `SCENARIO_ID(RecSide(F,G,H), AssLevelComb, ConsignmentModeId, AdditionalReqs#P,)` (plus FailureReasonId for negative scenarios).
- RecSide from {(1,0,0), (3,0,0), (3,1,2), (3,1,3)}.
- AdditionalReqs from {1,2}.

#### 8.1.3 Notation for 4 Corner and Extended Models Scenarios
- Notation includes parameters for ERD dispatch/receipt instances (metadata combos), receiving side, additional requirements.
- Coherence rules for (RelayMet#I, AssLevelComb, ConsignmentModeId):
  1. nil for indicators absent.
  2. For I∈{1,6}: both nil.
  3. For I∈{2,4,7}: AssLevelComb not nil, ConsignmentModeId nil.
  4. For I∈{3,5,8}: neither nil.
- Example of a specific test case provided.

#### 8.1.4 Reasons for Failures
- FailureReasonId values defined from ETSI EN 319 522-2:
  - XML_SUB_REJ: {RA02–RA06}
  - XML_REL_REJ: {RB02–RB10, RB21, RB22}
  - XML_CONS_FAIL, XML_CONS_NOT_FAIL: {RD03–RD06, RD21, RD32, RD34}
  - XML_CONT_HAND_FAIL: {RE03, RE04}
  - XML_CONS_REJ: RC08
  - XML_ACC_REJ_EXP: RC09

### 8.2 Test Cases for Black Box Model
- For each scenario: `SCENARIO_ID(RecSide(F,G,H), AdditionalReqs#P, FailureReasonId)`.
- Parameters as in 8.1.2.

### 8.3 Test Cases for 4-Corner and Extended Models
#### 8.3.1 General
Rules for building test case expressions from scenario parameters.

#### 8.3.2 Rules for Parametrizing ERD Dispatches
- Components: `Metadata(AS4Met#1, RelayMet#I, AssLevelComb, ConsignmentModeId), UserContent, <EVID>+`.
- I∈{1..8}, AssLevelComb∈{nil, low/low, substantial/substantial, high/high}, ConsignmentModeId∈{nil, basic, consented, consentedSigned}.

#### 8.3.3 Rules for Parametrizing ERD Payloads
- Same as dispatch but without evidence payloads.

#### 8.3.4 Rules for Parametrizing ERDS Receipts
- Components: `Metadata(AS4Met#1, RelayMet#I, AssLevelComb, nil), <EVID>+`.
- I and AssLevelComb as above.

#### 8.3.5 Rules for Parametrizing Entities at Receiving Side
- `RecSide(F,G,H)` with constraints from 7.3.

#### 8.3.6 Rules for Parametrizing Failure Reasons
- `FailureReasonId` as per 8.1.4.

#### 8.3.7 Rules for Parametrizing Additional Requirements
- `AdditionalReqs#P` with P∈{1,2}.
