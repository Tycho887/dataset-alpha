# ETSI TS 119 403-3 V1.1.1: Electronic Signatures and Infrastructures (ESI); Trust Service Provider Conformity Assessment; Part 3: Additional requirements for conformity assessment bodies assessing EU qualified trust service providers
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V1.1.1 | **Date**: 2019-03 | **Type**: Technical Specification (Normative)
**Original**: https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119400_119499/11940303/01.01.01_60/ts_11940303v010101p.pdf

## Scope (Summary)
This document specifies supplementary requirements to ETSI EN 319 403 for conformity assessment bodies (CABs) performing certification of qualified trust service providers (QTSPs) and the qualified trust services (QTSs) they provide against Regulation (EU) No 910/2014. It defines requirements for conformity assessment reports, including content.

## Requirements for CABs assessing QTSP/QTSs against requirements of Regulation (EU) No 910/2014

### 4.1 Conformity assessment scheme
- **CAS-4.1-01**: The conformity assessment scheme for which a CAB is accredited to assess QTSP/QTSs against Regulation (EU) No 910/2014 in accordance with Regulation (EC) No 765/2008 shall be defined such that accreditation ensures the CAB is competent to carry out conformity assessment of a QTSP/QTS against Regulation (EU) No 910/2014.
- **CAS-4.1-02**: The conformity assessment scheme shall, with the aim of confirming that the assessed QTSP/QTS fulfils applicable requirements from Regulation (EU) No 910/2014, include:
  a) requirements on the CAB, including auditing rules and effective set of criteria, meeting at least requirements from ETSI EN 319 403 [1];
     NOTE: This de facto implies the CAB being compliant with ETSI EN 319 403 [1], hence with ISO/IEC 17065 [i.30].
  b) control objectives and controls against which the CAB will assess a QTSP/QTS against applicable requirements of Regulation (EU) No 910/2014.
- **CAS-4.1-03**: The resulting conformity assessment report shall meet the requirements of clause 4.2.

### 4.2 Conformity assessment report
- **CAR-4.2-01**: The CAR shall bear a clear certification decision conformant to clause 7.6 of ETSI EN 319 403 [1], confirming – if such is the case – that the assessed QTSP/QTS meet all applicable requirements of Regulation (EU) No 910/2014.
- **CAR-4.2-02**: The CAR shall provide sufficient details to demonstrate fulfilment of all applicable requirements laid down in Regulation (EU) No 910/2014.
  NOTE 1: Details can be included by reference to other reports (e.g., audit reports against technical standards) and/or to security documentation.
- **CAR-4.2-03**: The CAR shall identify the name of the CAB, its registration number (where applicable), official postal address, and electronic address.
- **CAR-4.2-04**: The CAR shall identify:
  a) the name of the NAB having accredited the CAB;
  b) the link on the official NAB website to the accreditation certificate;
  c) when not in the accreditation certificate:
     i) the scheme under which the CAB has been accredited (e.g., ISO/IEC 17065 [i.30] completed by ETSI EN 319 403 [1]);
     ii) the conformity assessment (certification) scheme for which the CAB has been accredited.
- **CAR-4.2-05**: The complete CAR shall be part of the certification documentation specified in clause 7.7 of ETSI EN 319 403 [1].
- **CAR-4.2-06**: The CAR shall include the accredited conformity assessment (certification) scheme document (or set) or a link to its location.
- **CAR-4.2-07**: The CAR shall bear EU qualified electronic signature(s) identifying the name and title of the CAB responsible person(s) authorizing the certification decision.
- **CAR-4.2-08**: The CAR shall be dedicated to one and only one QTSP:
  a) identify the QTSP name, registration number, official postal and electronic address;
  b) when applicable, identify same information for all subsidiaries, affiliated legal entities, and (sub)contractors operating trust service components in scope.
  NOTE 2: QTSPs may use third parties; the QTSP remains responsible and liable.
- **CAR-4.2-09**: The CAR shall identify, per type of QTS, the service digital identity(ies) in accordance with clause 5.5.3 of ETSI TS 119 612 [2], providing at least:
  a) Subject Key Identifier (IETF RFC 5280 [3]);
  b) Base64 PEM representation of the associated X.509v3 digital certificate;
  c) when applicable, indication whether specific sets of end-entity certificates are excluded and on which criteria;
  d) indication whether the service digital identity relates to an end-entity or a certification authority (issuing, intermediate, root);
  e) indication of how the service digital identity is used in context of the corresponding QTS.
- **CAR-4.2-10**: Per type of QTS and for all service digital identities, the CAR shall provide a detailed description of the PKI functional hierarchy to allow identification of service entries for the national trusted list, including:
  a) illustration of PKI hierarchy identifying root, intermediate, and issuing CAs and certification paths;
  b) identification of each CA via Subject Key Identifier (IETF RFC 5280 [3]);
  c) for each issuing CA, list of (policy) sets of certificates with:
     i) unambiguous identification criteria (certificate policy identifiers or other criteria per clause 5.5.9.2.2 of ETSI TS 119 612 [2]);
     ii) indication whether certificates are qualified or non-qualified;
     iii) indication of purpose (electronic signatures, seals, website authentication, or other).
- **CAR-4.2-11**: The CAR shall provide an indication of the corresponding content of the applicable national trusted list reflecting the assessment result.
- **CAR-4.2-12**: The CAR shall include an exhaustive list of third parties providing/operating QTS components, with name and site locations.
- **CAR-4.2-13**: The CAR shall detail which third parties and sites have been subject to audit and to what extent.
- **CAR-4.2-14**: The CAR shall identify exhaustive list of public and QTSP internal documents (with versioning) in scope, including:
  a) public documents:
     i) declaration of practices;
     ii) QTS policy(ies);
     iii) terms and conditions (subscriber agreements);
  b) internal documents:
     i) termination plan (Article 24.2(i) of Regulation (EU) No 910/2014);
     ii) risk assessment documentation (Article 19.1);
     iii) security and data breach notification plan (Article 19.2);
     iv) list of internal documents supporting declaration of practices;
  c) public or internal documents:
     i) memorandum and Articles of Association;
     ii) evidence of sufficient financial resources and/or liability insurance;
     iii) list of standards with which operations are claimed compliant and audited/evaluated/certified compliant, with details of underlying scheme;
     iv) list of QSCDs and certification information (if delivered to users);
     v) list of trustworthy devices (e.g., HSMs) and certification information (if used).
- **CAR-4.2-15**: The CAR shall identify for each audit stage (e.g., documentation, implementation, onsite) the period and effort in man-days.
- **CAR-4.2-16**: The CAR shall provide for each of the following requirements of Regulation (EU) No 910/2014 an assessment report with indication of non-conformities and their criticality:
  a) General requirements (each with indication of relevant articles):
     i) Article 5 (data processing and protection) – reference may be made to assessment of Article 24.2(j);
     ii) Articles 13.2 and 13.3 (liability and burden of proof);
     iii) Article 15 (accessibility);
     iv) Article 19.1 (risk-based security);
     v) Article 19.2 (breach notification);
     vi) Article 20.1 (2-yearly audits and submission to SB);
     vii) Article 23 (EU trust mark, including CIR (EU) 2015/806 [i.8]);
     viii) points (a) to (j) of Article 24.2.
  b) Additional specific requirements per QTS type:
     i) Qualified certificates for electronic signatures: Articles 24.1(a)-(d), 24.2(k), 24.3, 24.4, 28.1 (including Annex I), 28.2, 28.3, 28.4, 28.5.
     ii) Qualified certificates for electronic seals: Articles 24.1(a)-(d), 24.2(k), 24.3, 24.4, 38.1 (including Annex III), 38.2, 38.3, 38.4, 38.5.
     iii) Qualified certificates for website authentication: Articles 24.1(a)-(d), 24.2(k), 24.3, 24.4, 45.1 (including Annex IV).
     iv) Qualified validation service for qualified electronic signatures: Articles 32.1, 32.2, 33.1.
     v) Qualified validation service for qualified electronic seals: Article 40.
     vi) Qualified preservation service for qualified electronic signatures: Article 34.1.
     vii) Qualified preservation service for qualified electronic seals: Article 40.
     viii) Qualified electronic time stamps: Article 42.1.
     ix) Qualified electronic registered delivery services: Article 44.1.
- **CAR-4.2-17**: The CAR shall identify in corresponding specific requirement reports the detailed audit controls and control objectives, with indication of each non-conformity and criticality, or include a reference to separately available audit reports if issued by an accredited CAB and endorsed by the CAB issuing the CAR.
  NOTE 3: Allows CARs built on separated "module-conformity-reports" for well-defined parts.
- **CAR-4.2-18**: The CAR shall include scope, description, and results of a significant set of test or production samples for all relevant and applicable types of outputs from the assessed QTS.
- **CAR-4.2-19**: When applicable, the CAR shall include assessment report against specific standards or publicly available specifications, with non-conformities and criticality.
  NOTE 4: Pending non-conformities acceptable only per clause 7.6 b) of ETSI EN 319 403 [1].
- **CAR-4.2-20**: The CAR shall indicate:
  a) by when the next surveillance audit must be conducted;
  b) by when the next compliance audit must be conducted.
- **CAR-4.2-21**: The CAR shall indicate under which circumstances a CAB accredited under Article 3.18 must be involved in reassessment in addition to planned audits.
- **CAR-4.2-22**: The CAR shall contain an explicit declaration stating that certification documents, including the CAR, are intended for use by SBs as defined in Regulation (EU) No 910/2014.

## Informative Annexes (Condensed)
- **Annex A (informative): QTSP/QTS conformity assessment against Regulation (EU) No 910/2014**: Explains that CABs must be accredited per Article 3.18 of the eIDAS Regulation. Conformity with standards alone is insufficient; CARs must demonstrate compliance with all applicable eIDAS requirements. A table mapping qualified trust services to relevant ETSI standards is provided (e.g., qualified certificates for electronic signatures to ETSI EN 319 411-2). Additional standards for remote signature creation (e.g., CEN EN 419 221-5, ETSI TS 119 431-1) are listed.
- **Annex B (informative): Bibliography**: Lists two ENISA guidelines on supervision and termination of qualified trust services (December 2017).
