# ETSI TS 119 431-2 V1.2.1 (2023-06): Policy and security requirements for trust service providers; Part 2: TSP service components supporting AdES digital signature creation
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V1.2.1 | **Date**: June 2023 | **Type**: Normative (with informative annexes)
**Original**: Provided text (ETSI TS 119 431-2)

## Scope (Summary)
This document specifies policy and security requirements for Trust Service Providers (TSPs) implementing a Signature Creation Application Service Component (SCASC) that supports AdES digital signature creation. It addresses service elements around the signature creation application (SCA) and does not restrict signing key management (may use remote server signing or local Signature Creation Device). Applicable to European and other regulatory frameworks, including Regulation (EU) No 910/2014 for advanced/qualified signatures and seals. Annex B provides EU-specific best practice requirements for X.509 certificates.

## Definitions and Abbreviations
- **AdES (digital) signature**: digital signature that is either a CAdES, PAdES, or XAdES signature.
- **digital signature**: data appended to, or a cryptographic transformation of a data unit that allows a recipient to prove source and integrity and protect against forgery.
- **digital signature value**: result of the cryptographic transformation that allows proof of source and integrity.
- **remote signature creation device**: SCDev used remotely from signer perspective; provides control of signing operation on signer's behalf.
- **server signing application**: application using a remote SCDev to create a digital signature value on behalf of a signer.
- **server signing application service component (SSASC)**: TSP service component employing a server signing application.
- **server signing application service provider (SSASP)**: TSP operating an SSASC.
- **signature applicability rules**: set of rules defining requirements for determination of whether a signature is fit for a particular business or legal purpose.
- **signature creation application (SCA)**: application within the signature creation system that creates the AdES digital signature and relies on the SCDev to create a digital signature value.
- **signature creation application service component (SCASC)**: TSP service component employing an SCA.
- **signature creation application service provider (SCASP)**: TSP operating an SCASC.
- **signature creation constraint**: criteria used when creating a digital signature.
- **signature creation device (SCDev)**: configured software or hardware used to implement signature creation data and to create a digital signature value.
- **signature creation policy**: set of signature creation constraints processed or to be processed by the SCA.
- **signature creation service**: TSP service implementing an SCA and/or a server signing application.
- **signature creation service provider**: service provider offering a signature creation service.

**Abbreviations**: CA (Certification Authority), DTBSR (Data To Be Signed Representation), OID (Object IDentifier), QES (Qualified Electronic Signature/Seal), SCA (Signature Creation Application), SCASC, SCASP, SCDev, SD (Signer's Document), SLA (Service-Level Agreement), SSASC, SSASP, TSA (Time-Stamping Authority), URI (Uniform Resource Identifier).

## 4 General Concepts

### 4.1 General policy requirements concepts
The document is structured in line with ETSI EN 319 401 [9] and incorporates its requirements by reference, adding requirements relevant for a SCASP.

### 4.2 Signature creation application service component applicable documentation

#### 4.2.1 SCASC practice statement
The SCASP develops, implements, enforces, and updates a SCASC practice statement (trust service practice statement per ETSI EN 319 401 [9]). It describes how the SCASP operates its service. Recipients: auditors, subscribers, relying parties. The present document provides high-level policy requirements to be reflected in the practice statement.

#### 4.2.2 SCASC policy
A SCASC policy describes what is offered independently of specific operating environment. The present document can be referred to by such a policy.
- OID for conformance to main normative requirements (excluding Annex B): `itu-t(0) identified-organization(4) etsi(0) CREATION SERVICE-policies(19431) ades (2) policy-identifiers(1) main (1)`
- OID for conformance including Annex B: `itu-t(0) identified-organization(4) etsi(0) CREATION SERVICE-policies(19431) ades (2) policy-identifiers(1) eu-advanced-x509 (2)`
A SCASC policy is not necessarily part of the SCASP's documentation; minimal information about offered service must be provided per clauses 6.1 and 6.2.

#### 4.2.3 Terms and conditions
The SCASP issues terms and conditions (see clause 6.2). They cover commercial/technical terms, are specific to the SCASP, and recipients may be subscribers and relying parties.

#### 4.2.4 Other documents associated with signature creation
- **Signature creation policy**: set of constraints processed by the SCA, identified by OID.
- **Signature applicability rules**: can include signature creation policy and other criteria for business fit (structured per ETSI TS 119 172-1 [i.3]).
SCASC practice statement differs from signature creation policy and signature applicability rules; owner of practice statement is SCASP, owner of applicability rules is typically signatory.

### 4.3 Architecture
The SCASC receives document(s)/hash(es) and signing parameters, collects information, prepares DTBSR, sends to SCDev (in user's environment or remote SSASC per ETSI TS 119 431-1 [i.8]). SCDev handles authentication and agreement to sign; returns digital signature value. SCASC includes value into digital signature. (See Figure 1 in original.)

## 6 Policies and practices

### 6.1 Trust service practice statement
- **OVR-6.1-01**: The requirements specified in ETSI EN 319 401 [9], clause 6.1 shall apply.
- **OVR-6.1-02**: [CONDITIONAL] When the SCASC supports inclusion of time-stamp tokens, the SCASC practice statement shall list which TSAs are used.
- **OVR-6.1-03**: The SCASC practice statement shall specify all the supported signature creation policies.
- **OVR-6.1-04**: The SCASC practice statement shall specify all the supported signature formats.
- **OVR-6.1-05**: The SCASC practice statement shall specify all the supported signature classes.
- **OVR-6.1-06**: The SCASP shall identify in the SCASC practice statement the obligations of all external organizations supporting its services including applicable policies and practices.

### 6.2 Terms and Conditions
- **OVR-6.2-01**: The requirements specified in ETSI EN 319 401 [9], clause 6.2 shall apply.
- **OVR-6.2-02**: The SCASC terms and conditions shall list or make reference to (e.g., through OIDs) and briefly describe the supported SCASC policies it conforms to.
- **OVR-6.2-03**: The SCASC terms and conditions may use the OIDs defined in clause 4.2.2.
- **OVR-6.2-03A**: [Conditional] If referencing a trust service policy using an OID other than those in clause 4.2.2, then the referenced policy shall comply with requirements in clause 9.
- **OVR-6.2-04**: The main OID (clause 4.2.2) shall only be used if the SCASC conforms to the normative requirements in the main part (excluding Annex B).
- **OVR-6.2-05**: The eu-advanced-x509 OID shall only be used if the SCASC conforms to the main part and Annex B.
- **OVR-6.2-06**: The terms and conditions shall indicate the rights and obligations of the SCASP and the signer.
- **OVR-6.2-07**: The terms and conditions shall describe the supported options. At least:
  a) supported signature formats (e.g., CAdES, XAdES, PAdES);
  b) supported signature parameters;
  c) if the document can be provided only as a hash;
  d) supported SCDevs or SSASCs.
- **OVR-6.2-08**: The terms and conditions shall include SLA elements for availability and, when applicable, other SLA information such as response times.
- **OVR-6.2-09**: The terms and conditions shall provide a notice that the SLA can be affected by practices/policies/SLAs of other TSPs not under the SCASP's control (e.g., CA, TSA).
- **OVR-6.2-10**: The terms and conditions shall explain how the SCASP processes personal data.

### 6.3 Information security policy
- **OVR-6.3-01**: The requirements specified in ETSI EN 319 401 [9], clause 6.3 shall apply.
- **OVR-6.3-02**: The security policy should document the security and privacy controls implemented to protect personal data.
  - NOTE: If the SCASP has access to the data-to-be-signed, this can contain confidential/personal data.

### 7.13 Compliance and legal requirements
- **OVR-7.13-01**: The requirements specified in ETSI EN 319 401 [9], clause 7.13 shall apply.
- **OVR-7.13-02**: When personal data is processed by a third party, if required by law, an appropriate agreement shall be made with third-party processors to ensure compliance, including technical, organizational, and legal measures to protect personal data.
  - NOTE: The data to be signed is to be considered as personal data.
- **OVR-7.13-03**: The SCASC shall not store the SD after processing when not necessary.
  - NOTE: If combined with a preservation service, there may be a need to keep such data.
- **OVR-7.13-04**: The SCASP shall have overall responsibility for meeting requirements of clauses 5 to 8 even when functionalities are sub-contracted.

## 8 Signature creation application service component technical requirements

### 8.1 Interface
- **ASI-8.1-01**: [CONDITIONAL] When the SCASC has a machine-accessible interface, it should use the protocol defined in ETSI TS 119 432 [i.9].
- **ASI-8.1-02**: The connection between the SCASC and the SCDev used for creation of the digital signature value shall be secured.
- **ASI-8.1-03**: [CONDITIONAL] When the SCASC presents the document to the signer, it shall describe in its SCASC practice statement how it guarantees What You See Is What You Sign (WYSIWYS).
- **ASI-8.1-04**: [CONDITIONAL] When the SCASC presents the document in an interpreted way, the SCASC practice statement shall clearly state how it interprets specific data (e.g., XML tags).
- **ASI-8.1-05**: [CONDITIONAL] When presenting the document, the SCASC practice statement or terms and conditions shall state which content types can be correctly presented.
- **ASI-8.1-06**: [CONDITIONAL] When presenting, the interface shall warn the signer if it cannot accurately present all parts of the SD according to the data content type.
- **ASI-8.1-07**: [CONDITIONAL] When providing a graphical user interface, requirements UI 1 and UI 2 from ETSI TS 119 101 [1] should apply.
- **ASI-8.1-08**: [CONDITIONAL] When presenting the document, the SCASC shall have a workflow where it is clear that the signer consents to signing.
- **ASI-8.1-09**: [CONDITIONAL] When presenting the document, SCP 13 and SCP 47 of ETSI TS 119 101 [1] shall apply.
- **ASI-8.1-10**: [CONDITIONAL] When presenting, the SCASC should allow downloading of the document to be signed.
- **ASI-8.1-11**: [CONDITIONAL] When presenting, the SCASC should log how long the document was presented.
- **ASI-8.1-12**: [CONDITIONAL] When presenting and the document was downloaded, the SCASC should log such an event.

### 8.2 AdES digital signature creation
- **OVR-8.2-01**: The SCASC shall guarantee the integrity and confidentiality of the received information.
- **OVR-8.2-02**: The cryptographic algorithms used should be selected from those recommended by ETSI TS 119 312 [i.5].
  - NOTE: National recommendations may supersede.
- **OVR-8.2-03**: The cryptographic algorithms applied shall be as defined in the signature creation policy.
- **OVR-8.2-04**: SCP 14, SCP 31, SCP 37, and SCP 61 of ETSI TS 119 101 [1] shall apply.
- **OVR-8.2-05**: The SCASC shall inform the signer of the commitment type.
  - NOTE: This can be given within the signature policy.
- **OVR-8.2-06**: The SCASC should include the signing certificate chain into the signature.
- **OVR-8.2-07**: The signer shall be able to know which signature creation policy will be applied.
- **OVR-8.2-08**: The signer shall be able to know which signature creation policy was applied for a specific signature.
  - Examples: user account info, signed attribute, or time-based single policy.
- **OVR-8.2-08A**: The SCASC should provide the signature to the signer.
- **OVR-8.2-09**: [CONDITIONAL] If the SCASC has access to the signed data, it should provide the signed data together with the signature to the signer.
  - NOTE: If signature is enveloped/enveloping, then OVR-8.2-09 follows from OVR-8.2-08A.

## 9 Framework for definition of SCASC policy built on a trust service policy defined in the present document
- **OVR-9-01**: Void.
- **OVR-9-01A**: [CONDITIONAL] When building a SCASC policy on a trust service policy defined herein, the SCASC policy shall identify which trust service policy it adopts as basis.
- **OVR-9-02**: [CONDITIONAL] The policy shall identify any variances it chooses to apply.
- **OVR-9-03**: [CONDITIONAL] Subscribers shall be informed, as part of implementing the terms and conditions, of ways the specific policy adds to or constrains the requirements of the base policy.
- **OVR-9-04**: [CONDITIONAL] There shall be a body (e.g., policy management authority) with final authority and responsibility for specifying and approving the policy.
- **OVR-9-05**: [CONDITIONAL] A risk assessment should be carried out to evaluate business requirements and determine security requirements.
- **OVR-9-06**: [CONDITIONAL] The policy shall be approved and modified in accordance with a defined review process, including responsibilities for maintaining the policy.
- **OVR-9-07**: [CONDITIONAL] A defined review process shall exist to ensure the policy is supported by the practice statements.
- **OVR-9-08**: [CONDITIONAL] The TSP should make available the supported policies to its user community.
- **OVR-9-09**: [CONDITIONAL] Revisions to policies should be made available to subscribers.
- **OVR-9-10**: [CONDITIONAL] A unique object identifier (e.g., OID or URI) shall be obtained for the policy.
