# ETSI TS 119 403: Trust Service Provider Conformity Assessment – Requirements for conformity assessment bodies assessing Trust Service Providers
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V2.2.1 | **Date**: 2015-08 | **Type**: Technical Specification (Normative)
**Original**: RTS/ESI-0019403-TSv221

## Scope
Contains requirements for the competence, consistent operation and impartiality of conformity assessment bodies (CABs) assessing and certifying TSPs and the trust services they provide against defined criteria. Applies ISO/IEC 17065 [1] to TSP-specific assessment.

## Definitions and Abbreviations
- **auditor**: person who assesses conformity to requirements as specified in a given requirements document
- **competence**: ability to apply knowledge and skills to achieve intended results
- **conformity assessment**: process demonstrating whether specified requirements relating to a product, process, service, system, person or body have been fulfilled (from [i.4] and [i.5])
- **conformity assessment body (CAB)**: body that performs conformity assessment services which is accredited as competent to carry out conformity assessment of a qualified TSP and the qualified trust services it provides (equivalent to point 13 Article 2 of [i.4])
- **national accreditation body**: sole body in a State that performs accreditation with authority derived from the State (as per [i.4])
- **technical expert**: person who provides specific knowledge or expertise to the audit team (not acting as auditor)
- **trust service**: electronic service which enhances trust and confidence in electronic transactions (typically using cryptographic techniques or involving confidential material)
- **Trust Service Provider (TSP)**: entity which provides one or more electronic trust services
- **Abbreviations**: CA, CAB, EC, EU, ISMS, IT, TSP

## 4 General Requirements
### 4.1 Legal and contractual matters
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clauses 4.1.1, 4.1.2, 4.1.3 shall apply.

### 4.2 Management of impartiality
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 4.2 shall apply. Additionally:
- **4.2.1 Activities not conflicting with impartiality**: CAB and its personnel may carry out additional activities provided they do not constitute a risk to impartiality. These may include organizing/participating in information meetings, training courses (lecturers shall confine to generic publicly available information), pre-audit readiness determination (shall not result in recommendations or reduce audit duration), performing third party audits under other schemes, adding value during audits (e.g., identifying opportunities for improvement without recommending specific solutions).

### 4.3–4.6
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clauses 4.3, 4.4, 4.5, 4.6 shall apply.

## 5 Structural Requirements
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clauses 5.1 and 5.2 shall apply.

## 6 Resource Requirements
### 6.1 CAB personnel
#### 6.1.1 General
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 6.1.1 shall apply.

#### 6.1.2 Management of competence for audit process
##### 6.1.2.0
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 6.1.2 shall apply.
##### 6.1.2.1 Management of competence
The performance of the following functions shall need specific TSP competences as described in clause 6.2:
- a) application review
- b) audit
- c) review
- d) certification decision
##### 6.1.2.2 Training of audit teams
CAB shall have criteria for training that support competence in:
- a) knowledge of TSP standards and other relevant publicly available specifications
- b) understanding of trust services and information security including network security
- c) understanding of risk assessment and management from business perspective
- d) technical knowledge of activity to be audited
- e) general knowledge of regulatory requirements relevant to TSPs
- f) knowledge of security policies and controls

### 6.2 Resources for evaluation
#### 6.2.0 General
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 6.2 shall apply.

#### 6.2.1 Internal resources
##### 6.2.1.0
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 6.2.1 shall apply. Additionally:
##### 6.2.1.1 Competence of CAB personnel
CAB shall have personnel competent to:
- a) select and verify competence of TSP auditors for audit teams
- b) brief TSP auditors and arrange necessary training
- c) decide on granting, maintaining, withdrawing, suspending, extending, or reducing certifications
- d) set up and operate appeals and complaints process

##### 6.2.1.2 Competences for all functions
Personnel shall have knowledge of:
- a) standards and publicly available specifications relevant to TSP conformity assessment
- b) TSPs general concepts and relevant requirements
- c) TSPs' legal and regulatory requirements
- d) trust services functioning, and information security management including network security
- e) TSPs' security policies and controls
- f) TSPs' risk assessment and risk management

##### 6.2.1.3 Competences for application review
Personnel reviewing TSP applications shall have:
- a) technological and legal understanding of TSP activity areas and associated business risks
- b) technical understanding of evaluation process
- c) understanding of CAB competences and capabilities
- d) communication and analytic skills to explain certification requirements and resolve differences

##### 6.2.1.4 Competences and prerequisites for auditing
Personnel performing audit (clause 7.4) shall fulfil:
- **Requirements**:
  - 1) formal academic/professional qualifications or extensive experience indicating capability
  - 2) at least four years full time practical IT experience, of which at least two in role relating to trust services, PKI, information security (including risk assessment/management), network security, and physical security
- **Knowledge of**:
  - 1) audit principles, practices and techniques in TSP audit gained in at least five-day training course
  - 2) issues related to trust services, PKI, information security, network security, physical security
  - 3) applicable standards, specifications, regulatory requirements for TSPs
  - 4) CAB's processes
- **Competences**:
  - 1) language skills appropriate for all organizational levels
  - 2) note-taking, report-writing, presentation, interviewing skills
  - 3) personal attributes: objective, mature, discerning, analytical, persistent, realistic

Personnel shall maintain competence; prior to assuming auditor role, candidate shall have gained experience under supervision of lead auditors in at least four TSP audits for total of at least 20 days (including documentation review, on-site audit, reporting).

##### 6.2.1.5 Competences for review
Personnel performing review shall have competences of clause 6.2.1.4 and have acted as auditor in at least three complete TSP assessments.

##### 6.2.1.6 Competences for certification decision
Personnel performing certification decision shall have competences of clause 6.2.1.2 and knowledge of certification process.

##### 6.2.1.7 Technical Experts
Technical experts assist audit team but are not auditors; need not have complete auditor competences. They shall be responsible to the audit team leader.

##### 6.2.1.8 Audit team
Audit teams shall be competent; at least one auditor in the team shall satisfy criteria for taking responsibility in:
- a) managing the team (lead auditor)
- b) demonstrated knowledge of legislative/regulatory requirements and legal compliance in TSP and information security
- c) current technical state-of-art regarding TSP and PKI
- d) technologies applicable to the TSP trust service being audited
- e) performing information security risk assessments to identify assets, threats, vulnerabilities, impact, mitigation
- f) organizational reliability issues

Team should be competent to trace security incidents back to TSP controls. Team may consist of one person if all criteria are met.

##### 6.2.1.9 Audit team leader
In addition to clause 6.2.1.4, audit team leaders shall have:
- a) acted as auditor in at least three complete TSP audits
- b) adequate knowledge and attributes to manage audit process
- c) competence to communicate effectively orally and in writing

## 7 Process Requirements
### 7.1 General
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.1 shall apply. The evaluation shall take form of audit against defined criteria that should:
- a) take into account specificities of trust service type
- b) ensure all aspects of TSP activity are fully covered
- c) be based on standards, publicly available specifications, and/or regulatory requirements

### 7.2 Application
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.2 shall apply.

### 7.3 Application Review
#### 7.3.0
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.3 shall apply. CAB shall ensure it does not accept applications for which it is incompetent. CAB shall demonstrate ability to:
- a) understand TSP activity areas and associated business risks
- b) define competencies needed for certification in relation to identified activities, trust services, security risks, vulnerabilities, impacts
- c) confirm availability of required competences

### 7.4 Audit
#### 7.4.0 General
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.4 shall apply.

#### 7.4.1 Audit Scope
##### 7.4.1.0
CAB documentation shall include policies and procedures for audit process, including checklists and procedures for assessing conformity. Audit team shall audit against all applicable requirements. CAB shall ensure scope and boundaries of TSP trust services are clearly defined. CAB shall ensure TSP's information security risk assessment properly reflects activities and extends to boundaries, including interfaces with services not completely within scope.

##### 7.4.1.1 Audit Team Mandate
Mandate shall be defined and made known to TSP, requiring examination of structure, policies, procedures, practices, management, operations, confirm they meet certification scope requirements and are implemented. Technical experts may complement but not replace auditors. CAB shall have procedure for selecting auditors/technical experts based on competence and for monitoring auditor performance.

##### 7.4.1.2 Audit Methodology
Plan and date agreed with TSP. Reporting procedures shall ensure:
- a) meeting prior to leaving premises with management, providing written/oral indication of conformity and opportunity for TSP questions
- b) team leader provides report of findings to CAB

CAB shall have procedures to verify TSP has established audit programme or passed other audits/certifications for different sites. Procedures shall not presuppose particular implementation manner or documentation format. Focus on establishing conformity. If network-assisted auditing used, TSP shall be made aware of security implications.

#### 7.4.2 Audit time
CAB shall allow sufficient time considering:
- a) size of trust service scope (number of systems, employees, certificates)
- b) complexity of trust service
- c) types of business performed
- d) extent and diversity of technology
- e) number of sites
- f) previously demonstrated performance
- g) extent of outsourcing and third party arrangements
- h) applicable standards, specifications, regulatory requirements
- i) existing certifications (e.g., ISO/IEC 27001, ISO/IEC 15408)

CAB shall document justification of time used.

#### 7.4.3 Multiple sites
##### 7.4.3.1 When to Consider Sample Based Approach
May use sample-based approach if TSP security management scheme ensures security administered under control of TSP's security policy and all sites subject to management review. Applicable sites are those directly concerned with TSP operations meeting specified policy requirements.

##### 7.4.3.2 Requirements of Sample Based Approach
CAB shall have procedures to ensure:
- a) initial contract review identifies site differences for adequate sampling
- b) representative number of sites sampled, considering: internal audit results, management review results, site size variations, business purpose variations, complexity of trust service and information systems, working practice variations, activity variations, potential interaction with critical/sensitive information systems, whether site operated by subcontractor, differing regulatory requirements
- c) sample partly selective based on (b) and partly non-selective, resulting in range of different sites without excluding random element
- d) every site subject to significant threats to assets, vulnerabilities, or impacts included in sampling programme
- e) surveillance programme designed to cover all sites within reasonable time unless demonstrated no impact on audit results
- f) nonconformity at head office or single site triggers corrective action applying to head office and all impacted sites

Audit shall address central site activities to ensure central security administration applied to all sites. CAB shall substantiate number of sites audited.

#### 7.4.4 Audit Report
##### 7.4.4.1 Report contents
Report provided to TSP and any party with legal reason shall contain:
- a) account of audit including document review summary and standards/specifications/regulatory requirements
- b) account of audit of TSP's information security risk analysis
- c) total audit time and detailed specification of time spent on document review, risk analysis assessment, on-site audit, reporting
- d) audit enquiries followed, rationale for selection, methodology including sampling and test procedures

##### 7.4.4.2 Report contents details
Report to CAB for certification decision shall contain:
- a) areas covered, certification requirements, sites audited, significant audit trails, methodologies
- b) observations (positive and negative)
- c) details of nonconformities with objective evidence and reference to criteria
- d) comments on conformity together with statement of nonconformity and comparison with previous audits

Completed questionnaires, checklists, observations, logs, notes may form part of report; if used, shall be submitted as evidence. Information about samples evaluated should be included. Report shall consider adequacy of internal organization.

#### 7.4.5 Audit process
##### 7.4.5.1 General preparation for initial audit
CAB shall require TSP to make all necessary arrangements for audit, including access to areas, records, personnel (including subcontractors). Prior to on-site audit, TSP shall provide:
- a) general information about trust service and activities
- b) information about locations, sizes, functions of TSP and subcontractor sites providing trust service
- c) copy of documentation on policies and practices governing trust service, including IT network infrastructure plans, manuals, instructions

##### 7.4.5.2 Audit Process
Objective: confirm TSP and trust services comply with assessment criteria. Auditors shall review before audit what records are confidential/sensitive; if effective audit not warranted, CAB shall inform TSP that audit can only take place when appropriate access arrangements accepted. Audit includes visits to TSP sites (see 7.4.3). Audit process shall be in at least two stages.

##### 7.4.5.3 Stage 1 audit
Auditors obtain and review documentation on TSP and audited service(s); make TSP aware of further information needed. CAB also obtains documentation on design of trust service. Objectives: provide focus for stage 2 planning by understanding structure and extent of TSP's audited service(s). Includes document review and may include verification of legal entity, liability arrangements, contractual relationships, internal/external audits, management review. Reports shall contain:
- a) description of organizational structure including subcontractors
- b) brief summary of document review
- c) account of audit of information security risk analysis
- d) brief assessment whether stage 2 likely to succeed and additional resources needed
- e) audit time spent on document review
- f) areas of concern on meeting requirements
- g) audit methodology employed

Document review shall be completed before stage 2. Results documented in written report including recommendations for stage 2. Concerns communicated to client. Interval between stages shall consider client needs to resolve concerns.

##### 7.4.5.4 Stage 2 audit
Always takes place at TSP sites. Auditors draft audit plan based on stage 1 observations. Objectives:
- a) confirm TSP adheres to its own policies, objectives, procedures
- b) confirm implemented trust services conform to applicable audit criteria and abide by TSP policies, objectives, procedures

Focus on collecting evidence on:
- a) implementation of trust service requirements
- b) trust service related organizational processes and procedures
- c) trust service related technical processes and procedures
- d) implemented information security measures including IT network protection
- e) trust service related products (trustworthy systems) such as cryptographic modules
- f) physical security of relevant TSP sites

#### 7.4.6 Audit Frequency
Full (re-)assessment audit period shall be no greater than two years unless otherwise required by applicable legislation or commercial scheme. (Surveillance may be required at any time per clause 7.9.)

### 7.5 Review
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.5 shall apply.

### 7.6 Certification decision
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.6 shall apply. Decision can be:
- a) certified: audited trust service fulfils criteria
- b) conditional certification pending corrective actions within 3 months, provided non-conformities do not impact ability to meet intended service
- c) not certified

### 7.7 Certification documentation
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.7 shall apply. Scope shall include identification of relevant trust service policy(ies) and/or trust service(s). CAB shall provide client with documentation clearly conveying or permitting identification of national accreditation body.

### 7.8 Directory of certified products
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.8 shall apply. CAB shall maintain and make publicly accessible up-to-date information on certified TSPs and certified trust services.

### 7.9 Surveillance
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.9 shall apply. CAB shall define programme of periodic surveillance and re-assessment including on-site audits. At least one surveillance audit per year recommended. Surveillance shall include:
- a) review of actions on previous nonconformities
- b) review of multi-site sampling strategy if applied
- c) review of changes in documentation and TSP operation
- d) review of internal audits and management review
- e) treatment of complaints
- f) use of marks/references to conformity assessment
- g) review of public TSP statements (e.g., promotional material, website)

Surveillance audits need not be full system audits; shall consider previously applied multi-sampling strategy. Sample of records since previous audit shall be examined. Reports shall include clearing of previously revealed nonconformities.

### 7.10 Changes affecting certification
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1], clause 7.10 shall apply. Changes initiated by client may include major changes in TSP documentation, policies, objectives, procedures affecting trust service, or security relevant changes (e.g., changes of trustworthy systems, network security, physical infrastructure, new sites). CAB shall establish procedure with each client for notification and determination of appropriate conformity assessment activities before implementation. Full re-assessment should occur under:
- a) major changes to scope
- b) major changes to trust services provided under scope
- c) new trust service included in scope
- d) major changes of IT systems or business processes used by TSP
- e) major part of trust services moves to another location

### 7.11–7.13
Requirements from ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clauses 7.11, 7.12, 7.13 shall apply.
