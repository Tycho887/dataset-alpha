# ETSI TS 101 533-1 V1.3.1: Electronic Signatures and Infrastructures (ESI); Data Preservation Systems Security; Part 1: Requirements for Implementation and Management
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V1.3.1 | **Date**: 2012-04 | **Type**: Normative
**Original**: [ETSI TS 101 533-1](http://www.etsi.org)

## Scope (Summary)
Specifies Information Security Management System (ISMS) security requirements for Data Preservation Service Providers (DPSP) when implementing and managing a Data Preservation System (DPS) to provide trustable and reliable services. Based on ISO/IEC 27001, ISO/IEC 27002, and TS 102 573. Does not address document management (refer to ISO 14721, ISO 15489, etc.).

## Definitions and Abbreviations (Selected)
- **access**: right, opportunity, means of finding, using, or retrieving information (ISO 15489).
- **Advanced Electronic Signature (AdES)**: electronic signature meeting Directive 1999/93/EC requirements.
- **Data Preservation Service Provider (DPSP)**: legal or natural person providing one or more Data Preservation Services.
- **Data Preservation System (DPS)**: set of hardware, software, policies, procedures, etc.
- **Long term**: period long enough for concerns about changing technologies (ISO 14721).
- **Statement of Applicability (SoA)**: documented statement describing control objectives and controls applicable to the DPSP ISMS.
- **Abbreviations**: AdES, CA, CP, CRL, CSP, DPS, DPSP, ISMS, PKC, QC, QES, SCD, SoA, SSCD, TSA, TSP, TST, UTC, etc.

## 4 Overview
- DPSP shall comply with ISO/IEC 27001 (recommended certification) and also ISO 9000 and ISO/IEC 20000.
- Based on TS 102 573 policy [N+] with additional security measures.
- Long-term preservation validity at least ensures integrity of preserved data objects and accountability via reliable audit trail (e.g., AdES, TSTs).
- Risk Assessment outcome shall be taken into account and made available to assessors.
- May be used within OAIS and Records Management frameworks.

### 4.1 Preservation Service types
- Core Data Preservation Service shall be implemented by each DPSP.
- Extended Services may be provided; each marked [EXT].
- Additional services shall not conflict with this document.

### 4.2 User Community and Applicability
- For any DPSP as guidance to implement and manage DPS compliant with TS 102 573, ISO/IEC 27001, ISO/IEC 27002.

### 4.3 Conformance requirements
- DPSP shall achieve formal recognition of conformance to its SoA.
- Mandatory requirements for Core and Extended services shall be implemented unless exceptional conditions with documented management decision.
- Recommended provisions should be implemented; deviations must be justified in writing.
- SoA shall specify:
  a) which controls are selected/excluded with rationale;
  b) which controls from other public specifications are implemented with rationale.

## 5 Provisions based on TS 102 573
Provisions in TS 102 573 clauses apply with additions below.

### 5.1 Compliance with the present document provisions
- DPSP shall comply with all provisions taking into account its SoA.
- Transpose provisions into ISPD, Operational Procedures, job descriptions.
- Demonstrate compliance to assessors.

### 5.2 DPSP Obligations

#### 5.2.1 Arrangements to cover liabilities and financial stability
- Shall perform initial and periodic Risk Assessment (financially quantitative) to assess financial stability and liability coverage.
- Shall have financial capability via own assets, insurance, or both.
- Risk Assessment cycle includes evaluation of technical risks, financial exposure, insurance, and final acceptable exposure.

#### 5.2.2 Conformance by sub-contractors
- DPSP bears full responsibility for compliance even when functions are sub-contracted.
- Sub-contractors must agree to allow access to premises by DPSP personnel and assessors.

#### 5.2.3 DPSP service provisions in abidance by the applicable legislation
- Where conflict, applicable legislation prevails.
- DPSP shall be registered as legal entity; demonstrate compliance with applicable legislation.
- Agreement documentation shall specify legislation complied with, in official EU language(s).
- Auditable procedures for exhibition/delivery of preserved data to authorized entities.
- [EXT.1] Personal data preservation: abide by rules on transfer to third countries; metadata processed only under agreement.

#### 5.2.4 Contractual aspects
- Procedures to verify depositor identity.
- Agreement shall specify if DPSP may process data for metadata, which roles may access preserved objects.
- Procedures to return data objects to owners upon request or agreement closure in original or agreed format.
- Maximum time for exhibition/delivery shall be agreed.
- Deletion of preserved objects shall also delete backups, metadata, etc. Closure Evidence should be unaffected.
- Agreement shall specify media for return; DPSP shall provide authenticity/integrity measures (e.g., signatures).
- Specify personnel responsible for interfacing subscriber.
- Ensure delivery only to legitimately entitled persons.

#### 5.2.5 Resolution of complaints and disputes
- Auditable procedures to provide receipts for deposited batches suitable as evidence (e.g., with electronic signatures).
- Securely record evidence of reception and instructions for handling.
- [EXT1.1] Procedures to report personal data subject requests to controller.
- [EXT1.2] Procedures to facilitate data subject access within legal delays.

#### 5.2.6 Organisation independence
- DPSP shall be independent from customers and providers of signature certification, time stamping, time reference services, OR ensure decisions free from undue influence if part of same organisation.

#### 5.2.7 DPSP Subscriber Obligations
- TS 102 573 clause 6.3 applies except subscriber signing keys.
- Subscriber shall communicate identity of entitled persons and means to authenticate them.
- Agreement shall specify subscriber responsibility for content, media, format compliance.

#### 5.2.8 Information for trading partners
- TS 102 573 clause 6.4 applies except item a) covered in A.12.2.1.
- Subscribers shall be informed whether DPSP performs all functions in person or through external providers, and identification of external providers.

#### 5.2.9 Information for auditor/regulatory/tax authorities
- Auditable procedures to exhibit data objects with authenticity/integrity proof.
- Specify security measures authorities must abide by when accessing DPS.

## 6 Objectives and controls in TS 102 573, annex A
Provisions from TS 102 573 apply with additions.

### 6.1 SS.1. Signature
- Long-term preservation validity shall be assured by AdES or other mechanisms (TSTs).
- Note: electronic signatures used for integrity proof, not for commitment.

#### 6.1.1 SS.1.1. Class of Electronic Signature
- Derogation: electronic signature (especially on Closure Evidence) not required to be QES, but shall be at least AdES in compliance with applicable legislation.

#### 6.1.2 SS.1.2. Certification
- Certificates should be based on ISO/IEC 9594-8.
- DPSP shall inform subscriber about CAs, certificate policies, TSAs.

#### 6.1.3 SS.1.3. Signature Creation Data
- Key management activities shall be logged auditable. If SSCD used, comply with CP/CPS. If not, ensure SCD confidentiality.

#### 6.1.4 SS.1.4. Certificate subject's Registration
- Delegate signers shall be officially appointed.
- DS certificates shall be issued upon agreements with CAs; "Organization" field should reference DPSP.
- If QC, comply with TS 101 456 or recognized CP. If not QC, should comply with TS 102 042 or recognized CP.

#### 6.1.5 SS.1.5. Certificate Revocation
- Auditable procedures to request revocation.
- DS shall request revocation if private key compromised.
- DPSP shall request revocation upon compromise or cessation of subject.

### 6.2 SS.2. Maintenance of Signature over storage period
- DPSP shall ensure signatures (e.g., within Closure Evidence) maintain validity.
- Reliable time reference (TST, trusted time evidence, time-mark) should be applied close to signing time.
- Auditable procedures for maintaining signatures, collecting evidence (CRLs, OCSP responses).
- May derogate if legal/technical needs differ.

### 6.3 SS.3. Storage

#### 6.3.1 SS.3.1. Authorized Access
- Documented and auditable procedures to prevent unauthorized access.
- Modify/delete only upon valid request; keep audit trail.
- Remote access encryption system agreed and documented, unless required by law.
- Access shall not be all-encompassing.

#### 6.3.2 SS.3.2. Authenticity and Integrity
- DPSP should adopt Closure Evidence solution.
- Where used, document implementation based on public or custom specification.
- If not used, implement documented mechanisms achieving same objectives.
- Where Closure Evidence sealed with signature, DPSP states preservation performed per this document.
- Auditable procedures for algorithm migration.

#### 6.3.3 SS.3.3. Data Object Readability [EXT]
- To ensure readability, either store required software/hardware, or perform format conversion with trusted third party attestation.
- Auditable procedures to verify readability at intervals; if degradation, recreate from backup as incident.
- Agreement shall address notification if impossible to recreate.
- Business Continuity Management shall address software/hardware.

#### 6.3.4 SS.3.4. Storage media type
- DPSP may agree on media types with data owners among state of art.
- If media not lasting, have written plans and auditable procedures for migration.
- If media turn out not to last, migrate in time.

#### 6.3.5 SS.3.5. Data Objects Format
- Agreement shall specify analog and/or electronic formats.
- [EXT1] Verify format compliance with legal/customer requirements.
- [EXT2] Verify formats not prone to Presentation Corruption Agents.
- [EXT3] Verify absence of PCA; notify subscriber of faults; have evidence of capability.
- [EXT4] For XML, preserve all information concurring in presentation.

#### 6.3.6 SS.3.6. Requirements on Separation and Confidentiality
- Auditable procedures to ensure confidentiality by segregating assets of different owners, at least logical access separation.

### 6.4 SS.4. Reporting to and Exchanges with Authorities
- Auditable procedures for remote exhibition/submission via secured channels (e.g., TLS, end-to-end encryption) ensuring confidentiality of authentication and transmitted data.

### 6.5 SS.5. Conversion of Analog Originals to Digital Formats [EXT1]
- [EXT1.1.1] Auditable procedures to convert analog to electronic formats agreed with owner, known as immune from PCA.
- [EXT1.1.2] Procedures shall provide audit trail with operator identifier, inventory, failures, interventions, output count.
- [EXT1.1.3] Closure Evidence should be generated.
- [EXT1.2] For unique analog original, AdES should be applied; assertion from TS 102 573 associated.
- [EXT1.3] Make available Risk Assessment outcomes addressing conversion.

## Annex A (normative): ISO/IEC 27001 related Long Term Preservation-specific ISMS control objectives, controls and implementation guidance
Clauses A.5 to A.15 correspond to ISO/IEC 27002 clauses. Controls from ISO/IEC 27002 are recommended; each clause adds long-term preservation-specific controls (mandatory, recommended, or optional). Key additions include:

- **A.5.1.1**: ISPD shall cover data object protection commensurate with sensitivity; made available to all personnel.
- **A.5.1.2**: ISPD reviewed at least yearly and upon high-risk incidents; enforced with timeliness from Risk Assessment.
- **A.6.1.1**: Keep exhaustive auditable documentation on organisation.
- **A.6.1.3**: Clear chain of command; responsible persons identified.
- **A.6.1.5**: No disclosure without written authorisation and need-to-know.
- **A.6.2.3**: Third party agreements shall comply with data protection; require escrow for ad hoc software.
- **A.7.1.1**: Auditable inventory of data objects with history; include version and operational period.
- **A.7.2.1**: Data objects shall have classification level; DPSP shall abide by owner's classification.
- **A.7.2.2**: Labelling and handling procedures; copies receive same classification.
- **A.8.1.1**: System administrators appointed in writing.
- **A.8.1.3**: Officers shall declare awareness of rules and sanctions.
- **A.8.2.1**: Job descriptions formally communicated; management appoint experienced persons.
- **A.8.2.2**: Formal information security awareness; repeat upon policy changes.
- **A.9.1.1**: Computer rooms with safety doors and alarms; log events.
- **A.9.1.2**: Physical entry control; visitors supervised.
- **A.9.2.1**: Equipment sited to prevent electromagnetic capture.
- **A.9.2.2**: Emergency power supply for regular shutdown.
- **A.9.2.6**: Secure disposal or re-use; overwrite data.
- **A.10.1.1**: Documented procedures for storage logical unit content structure, history, backup info.
- **A.10.1.2**: Change management with formal authorisation; traceable.
- **A.10.1.3**: Segregation of duties (system administrators, operators, auditors).
- **A.10.1.4**: Separation of development, test, operational facilities.
- **A.10.2.2**: Appoint senior managers responsible for third party services.
- **A.10.2.3**: Agreements require timely information on changes.
- **A.10.3.2**: System acceptance with security requirements approval.
- **A.10.4.1**: Malware controls; shutdown and notify if infected; scanned before restore.
- **A.10.5.1**: Backup copies interoperable; equipment available for restore; periodic readability checks.
- **A.10.6.2**: Networks separated by firewalls; Intrusion Detection Systems; wireless encryption.
- **A.10.7.1**: Procedures for removable media management, inspections, degradation detection.
- **A.10.7.2**: Media disposal by secure deletion, incineration, or destruction.
- **A.10.8.1**: Encrypt exchanged information.
- **A.10.10.1**: Audit logging of security events; keep logs at least as long as legal/agreement requirement.
- **A.10.10.3**: Protect log information; replicate/backup daily.
- **A.10.10.4**: Notify operators that all activities are logged.
- **A.10.10.6**: Clock synchronization to UTC; all logs in consistent time.
- **A.11.1.1**: Access control policy differentiating user types (operators, administrators, owners, auditors, third parties).
- **A.11.2.1**: User registration for operators and administrators.
- **A.11.2.3**: Password management; lockout after failed attempts; no clear display.
- **A.11.2.4**: Review access rights upon security incidents.
- **A.11.3.1**: Password use rules; minimum length; user awareness.
- **A.11.3.3**: Clear desk and screen policy with inspections.
- **A.11.4.1**: Policy on network services; access only to trusted officers for backup.
- **A.11.4.2**: Two-factor authentication for external connections on sensitive data.
- **A.11.5.1**: Secure log-on with lockout.
- **A.11.5.3**: Passwords not hardcoded.
- **A.11.6.1**: Restrict access to logs and data objects to need-to-know.
- **A.11.6.2**: DPS components should be dedicated and isolated.
- **A.12.1.1**: Security requirements in COTS or developed systems.
- **A.12.2.1**: Input data validation; track states; mechanisms to verify alteration-free reception.
- **A.12.2.2**: Control internal processing; verify integrity at key steps.
- **A.12.3.1**: Use recognized cryptographic algorithms; monitor advances.
- **A.12.3.2**: Key management logged; emergency procedures for key recovery; dual control.
- **A.12.4.1**: Control operational software; sign-off after acceptance tests.
- **A.12.4.2**: Protect system test data; anonymize if used in testing.
- **A.12.5.1**: Change control with segregated test environment.
- **A.12.5.3**: Agreements to prevent supplier-triggered modifications.
- **A.12.6.1**: Teams keep up to date on security vulnerabilities.
- **A.13.1.1**: Instructions for reporting security events; classify severity; formal acceptance.
- **A.13.1.2**: Instructions for reporting security weaknesses.
- **A.13.2.1**: Response team with documented procedures; emergency actions recorded.
- **A.13.2.2**: Learning from incidents; trigger review of Risk Assessment/ISPD.
- **A.14.1.1**: Business Continuity Plan designed to resume activities without unacceptable delay.
- **A.14.1.2**: Risk assessment includes continuity impact analysis; define maximum down-time for key services.
- **A.14.1.3**: Identify acceptable loss of information and services.
- **A.14.1.4**: BCP specifies invocation authority; Disaster Recovery Team; recovery site under control of DPSP or reliable provider.
- **A.14.1.5**: Regular testing including technical recovery, alternate site, supplier facilities, complete rehearsals.
- **A.15.1.1**: Identify applicable legislation; satisfy ISO 15489 part 1 ch.5.
- **A.15.1.2**: Licensed hardware/software registered; document IPR waivers.
- **A.15.1.3**: Should comply with ISO 15489 records management requirements.
- **A.15.2.1**: Compliance reviews by managers; communicate consequences of non-compliance.
- **A.15.2.2**: Technical compliance checks; methodology for periodical review.
- **A.15.3.2**: Protect audit tools from unauthorized access; remove when not used.
