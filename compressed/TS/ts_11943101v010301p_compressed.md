# ETSI TS 119 431-1 V1.3.1: Electronic Signatures and Trust Infrastructures (ESI); Policy and security requirements for trust service providers; Part 1: TSP services operating a remote QSCD / SCDev
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V1.3.1 | **Date**: 2024-12 | **Type**: Technical Specification (Normative)
**Original**: ETSI TS 119 431-1 V1.3.1 (2024-12)

## Scope (Summary)
This document specifies policy and security requirements for Trust Service Providers (TSPs) operating a remote Signature Creation Device (SCDev), including a remote Qualified Signature/Seal Creation Device (QSCD) as defined in Regulation (EU) 2024/1183. It covers requirements for creation, maintenance, life-cycle management and use of signing keys. The service is termed Server Signing Application Service (SSAS). Specific requirements for qualified TSPs are in Annex A.

## 3 Definition of terms, symbols, abbreviations and notations
### 3.1 Terms (key)
- **authentication**: provision of assurance in the claimed identity of an entity (ISO/IEC 18014-2)
- **digital signature value**: result of cryptographic transformation
- **electronic identification (eID)**: process of using person identification data
- **electronic identification means**: material/immaterial unit containing person identification data used for authentication
- **one-time signing key**: key bound, certified, used and disposed based on a single authorization, linked to a single session signing DTBS/R(s)
- **person identification data**: set of data enabling identity establishment
- **Qualified electronic Signature/seal Creation Device (QSCD)**: as specified in Regulation (EU) No 910/2014
- **Remote QSCD**: as specified in Regulation (EU) 2024/1183
- **remote signature creation device**: device used remotely, provides control of signing operation on signer's behalf
- **Server Signing Application Service (SSAS)**: trust service consisting of a server signing application and a QSCD/SCDev to create digital signature value on behalf of a signer
- **Server Signing Application Service Provider (SSASP)**: TSP operating a SSAS
- **Signature Creation device (SCDev)**: configured software/hardware to implement signature creation data and create digital signature value
- **trust service**: electronic service that enhances trust
- **Trust Service Provider (TSP)**: entity which provides one or more trust service

## 6 Trust Service Providers practice
### 6.1 Publication and repository responsibilities
- **OVR-6.1-01**: TSP shall make available SPs, practice statements, terms and conditions.
- **OVR-6.1-02**: Terms and conditions shall be readily identifiable for a given signing key or certificate.
- **OVR-6.1-03**: Information shall be available 24/7; max downtime specified in practice statement.
- **OVR-6.1-04**: Should be publicly and internationally available.

### 6.2 Signing key initialization
#### 6.2.1 Signing key generation
- **GEN-6.2.1-01 [LSP]**: Clause SRG_KM.1.1 of EN 419241-1 shall apply.
- **GEN-6.2.1-02 [NSP]**: Clause SRA_SKM.1.1 of EN 419241-1 shall apply.
- **GEN-6.2.1-02A [NSP]**: Signer's signing key shall be generated and used in a SCDev certified conformant to EN 419221-5.
- **GEN-6.2.1-03**: Clause SRG_KM.1.2 (algorithms/key lengths) shall apply.
- **GEN-6.2.1-04**: Clause SRG_KM.1.3 (key protection) shall apply.
- **GEN-6.2.1-05**: Clause SRG_KM.1.4 (device initialization) shall apply.
- **GEN-6.2.1-06**: Clause SRC_SKS.1.1 (algorithm parameters) shall apply.
- **GEN-6.2.1-07**: Clause SRC_SKS.1.3 (time of generation) shall apply.
- **GEN-6.2.1-08 [CONDITIONAL]**: If SSAS and certificate generation are separate, SSAS shall support REG-6.3.1-01 of ETSI EN 319 411-1 (e.g., provide assertion linked to private key).
- **GEN-6.2.1-09 [CONDITIONAL]**: For one-time signing key, key shall be bound to exactly one signature session.

#### 6.2.2 eID means or identity linking
- **LNK-6.2.2-00 [CONDITIONAL]**: For one-time signing key, key and authentication may be linked directly to identity instead of eID means.
- **LNK-6.2.2-01A [LSP]**: Clause SRC_SA.1.1 (enrolment) shall apply.
- **LNK-6.2.2-02A [NSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: If signer is natural person, identity proofing shall be as per clause A.1.2 of EN 419241-1, assurance level substantial or higher.
- **LNK-6.2.2-02B [NSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: If legal person, as per clause A.1.3, substantial or higher.
- **LNK-6.2.2-02C [NSP]**: Application and registration during enrolment shall be as per clause A.1.1 of EN 419241-1.
- **LNK-6.2.2-02D [NSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: If authentication linked to eID means, eID means characteristics shall be as per clause A.2.1, substantial or higher.
- **LNK-6.2.2-02E [NSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: Authentication mechanism shall be as per clause A.2.2, substantial or higher.
- **LNK-6.2.2-03A [CONDITIONAL]**: If linked to eID means, SSASP shall link signing keys with appropriate eID means reference.
- **LNK-6.2.2-03B [CONDITIONAL]**: If linked directly to identity, SSASP shall link one-time signing key with specific identity.
- **LNK-6.2.2-04**: SSASP may generate eID means reference and provide eID means (see 6.2.4).
- **LNK-6.2.2-05**: SSASP shall ensure person identification data linked to eID means reference or identity is same as subject of associated certificate.
- **LNK-6.2.2-06**: eID means reference may be provided by external party.
- **LNK-6.2.2-07A [LSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: If authentication delegated, SSASP shall ensure external party meets LNK-6.2.2-01A and LNK-6.2.2-03A or -03B. Notified eID/EUDI wallet may be assumed conformant.
- **LNK-6.2.2-08 [NSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: If authentication delegated, SSASP shall ensure external party meets LNK-6.2.2-02A, -02B, -02C, -02D, -02E, and -03A or -03B.
- **LNK-6.2.2-08A [NSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: If all authentication delegated, SSASP shall ensure secret key material of delegated party resides in certified cryptographic module per SRG_KM.1.1 of EN 419241-1.
- **LNK-6.2.2-09 [NSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: If delegation, SSASP shall ensure external party fulfills all relevant requirements or uses notified eID means.
- **LNK-6.2.2-10A**: SSASP shall protect integrity of links between signing key and eID means reference or identity.

#### 6.2.3 Certificate linking
- **LNK-6.2.3-01**: Clause SRC_SKS.1.2 (certificate linking) shall apply.
- **LNK-6.2.3-02**: Clause SRC_SKS.1.4 (certificate linking) shall apply.
- **LNK-6.2.3-03**: Clause SRC_SKS.1.5 (links protection) shall apply.

#### 6.2.4 eID means provision
- **EID-6.2.4-01 [CONDITIONAL]**: If SSASP provides eID means, it shall be securely passed to signer.
- **EID-6.2.4-02 [CONDITIONAL]**: If personalization with activation data (e.g., PIN), activation data shall be securely prepared and distributed separately from eID means.

### 6.3 Signing key life-cycle operational requirements
#### 6.3.1 Signature activation
- **SIG-6.3.1-01**: Clause SRC_SA.1.2 (authentication) shall apply.
- **SIG-6.3.1-02**: Clause SRC_SA.1.3 (protocol security) shall apply.
- **SIG-6.3.1-03**: Clause SRC_SA.1.4 (access control) shall apply.
- **SIG-6.3.1-04**: Clause SRC_SA.1.5 (signing key control) shall apply.
- **SIG-6.3.1-05 [NSP]**: Clause SRA_SKM.2.1 (signing key activation) shall apply.
- **SIG-6.3.1-06 [NSP]**: Clause SRA_SAP.1.2 (protocol security) shall apply.
- **SIG-6.3.1-07 [NSP]**: Clause SRA_SKM.2.5 (signing key control) shall apply.
- **SIG-6.3.1-08**: SSASP should ensure public key certificate is valid before using signing key.
- **SIG-6.3.1-09**: Signing keys shall be usable only when signer's consent has been obtained.
- **SIG-6.3.1-10**: Clause SRC_DSC.1.1 (signature creation algorithm parameters) shall apply.
- **SIG-6.3.1-11 [CONDITIONAL]**: If authentication linked directly to identity, SAD shall contain unique identifier of signature session uniquely linked to SSAS.
- **SIG-6.3.1-12 [CONDITIONAL]**: If authentication linked directly to identity, SAD shall contain unique identifier of identity verification process.
- **SIG-6.3.1-13 [CONDITIONAL]**: If authentication linked directly to identity, signature session shall be linked to exactly one SAD containing all documents, or multiple SAD values if consecutive signatures on same document.
- **SIG-6.3.1-14 [CONDITIONAL]**: If authentication linked directly to identity, signature session shall end at most 2 hours after identity verification process.
- **SIG-6.3.1-15 [NSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: If signer is natural person and authentication linked directly to identity, SAP shall include explicit action (not just checkbox) to approve authorization.
- **SIG-6.3.1-16 [NSP] [CONDITIONAL]**: If signer is legal person and authentication linked directly to identity, SAP shall include explicit action of natural person to approve authorization.

#### 6.3.2 Signing key deletion
- **DEL-6.3.2-01**: Clause SRG_KM.7.1 shall apply. If public key certificate revoked, corresponding signing key shall be destroyed.
- **DEL-6.3.2-02**: SSASP shall destroy signing key when requested by signer.
- **DEL-6.3.2-03**: Clause SRG_KM.7.2 (session management) shall apply.
- **DEL-6.3.2-04**: Clause SRG_KM.7.3 (key backup deletion) shall apply.
- **DEL-6.3.2-05 [CONDITIONAL]**: For one-time signing key, key shall be deleted immediately after end of signature session.

#### 6.3.3 Signing key backup and recovery
- **GEN-6.3.3-01**: Clause SRG_KM.2.1 (key backup) shall apply.
- **GEN-6.3.3-02**: Clause SRG_KM.2.2 (backup protection) shall apply.
- **GEN-6.3.3-03**: Clause SRG_KM.2.3 (backup controls) shall apply.
- **GEN-6.3.3-04**: Number of duplicated datasets shall not exceed minimum needed to ensure continuity.
