# ETSI TS 119 142-3 V1.1.1: Electronic Signatures and Infrastructures (ESI); PAdES digital signatures; Part 3: PAdES Document Time-stamp digital signatures (PAdES-DTS)
**Source**: ETSI / TC ESI | **Version**: V1.1.1 | **Date**: 2016-12 | **Type**: Normative Technical Specification
**Original**: [ETSI Direct Download](http://www.etsi.org/standards-search) (Reference DTS/ESI-000122)

## Scope (Summary)
Specifies a PAdES digital signature format (PAdES-DTS) based solely on a Document Time‑stamp (as defined in ETSI EN 319 142‑1 [2]) to prove integrity and existence of a PDF document (per ISO 32000‑1 [1]) **without** providing signer authentication or proof of origin. This format does **not** meet advanced electronic signature/seal requirements of Regulation (EU) No 910/2014 [i.4].

## Definitions and Abbreviations
- **digital signature**: data appended to, or a cryptographic transformation of a data unit that allows a recipient to prove source and integrity and protect against forgery.
- **digital signature value**: result of the cryptographic transformation that allows a recipient to prove source and integrity and protect against forgery.
- **PAdES signature**: digital signature satisfying requirements of ETSI EN 319 142‑1 [2] or ETSI EN 319 142‑2 [i.1].
- **proof of existence**: evidence that an object existed at a specific date/time.
- **proof of integrity**: evidence of accuracy and completeness of an object.
- **(electronic) time‑stamp**: data in electronic form that binds other electronic data to a particular time, establishing evidence of existence at that time (for IETF RFC 3161 [i.2] updated by RFC 5816 [i.3], refers to the *timeStampToken* field).

## 4 General Syntax
### 4.1 General Requirements for PAdES‑DTS Signatures
- PAdES‑DTS builds on PDF signatures (ISO 32000‑1 [1]) using a Document Time‑stamp instead of a signer certificate.
- Provides **only proof of integrity** of the PDF content and, with the Document Time‑stamp, **proof of existence** of the document.
- **Shall apply**:
  - a) The Signature Dictionary **shall** be a Document Time‑stamp Dictionary per clause 5.4.3 of ETSI EN 319 142‑1 [2].
  - b) All requirements of clause 5.4.3 of ETSI EN 319 142‑1 [2] **shall** apply.
  - c) Other PDF signature handling requirements from ISO 32000‑1 clause 12.8 **shall** apply except as overridden by this document.

### 4.2 Extending the Validity of PAdES‑DTS Signatures
- The protection lifetime **may** be extended beyond the underlying Document time‑stamp by adding a new Document time‑stamp and DSS information as described in ETSI EN 319 142‑1 [2], clause 5.4.

## 5 Attributes Syntax and Semantics
### 5.1 Extensions Dictionary
- **Should include** entry `<< /ESIC <</BaseVersion /1.7 /ExtensionLevel 1 >> >>` (clause 4.1).
- Alternative: Adobe® equivalent `<< /ADBE <</BaseVersion /1.7 /ExtensionLevel 8 >> >>`.

### 5.2 Requirements on Encryption
- If encryption and signatures are combined, encryption **shall** be applied as described in ETSI EN 319 142‑1 [2], clause 5.5.

## 6 PAdES‑DTS Signature Profiles
### 6.1 Signature Levels
- **PAdES‑DTS‑BET**: basic level providing proof of existence and integrity.
- **PAdES‑DTS‑A**: incorporates additional time‑stamps for long‑term validation (availability and integrity of validation material).

### 6.2 General Requirements
#### 6.2.1 Requirements from Part 1
- Requirements from clauses 4.1, 5.3, 5.4, 5.5 and 6.2.1 of ETSI EN 319 142‑1 [2] **shall** apply to all levels.

**Additional requirements**:
- a) SubFilter value **shall** be `ETSI.RFC3161`.
- b) The signature **shall** contain a document security store (DSS) as defined in clause 5.4.2 of ETSI EN 319 142‑1 [2] followed by a document time‑stamp per clause 5.4.3 of that standard.
- c) A new Document time‑stamp and DSS information **shall** be added as described in clause 5.4 of ETSI EN 319 142‑1 [2].
- d) Applications **should** include certificate values within the DSS. The full set of certificates (including the trust anchor, if available as a certificate) used to validate the signature and not already present **shall** be included. This includes certificates for validating any TSA certificate.
- e) Duplication of certificate values within the signature **should** be avoided.
- f) The full set of revocation data (CRL or OCSP responses) used in validation of signer and CA certificates (including those for TSA certificates) **shall** be included.
- g) DER encoding **shall** be used for certificate‑values and revocation‑values.
- h) The VRI dictionary **should not** be used; inclusion is optional. All validation material referenced in VRI entries is also referenced in DSS entries.
- i) PAdES‑DTS‑A signatures **may** have more than one document‑time‑stamp applied after DSS and DSS/VRI.
- j) Before generating a document‑time‑stamp attribute, applications **shall** include all validation material not already in the timestamp required for validating that timestamp (certificates and certificate status information). This material **should** be incorporated within DSS.
