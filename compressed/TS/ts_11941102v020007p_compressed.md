# ETSI TS 119 411-2 V2.0.7: Electronic Signatures and Infrastructures (ESI); Policy and security requirements for Trust Service Providers issuing certificates; Part 2: Requirements for trust service providers issuing EU qualified certificates
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V2.0.7 | **Date**: 2015-07 | **Type**: Normative
**Original**: [ETSI TS 119 411-2 v2.0.7 PDF](http://www.etsi.org/standards-search)

## Scope (Summary)
The present document specifies policy and security requirements for the issuance, maintenance and life-cycle management of EU qualified certificates as defined in Regulation (EU) N° 910/2014 [i.1]. It defines five certificate policies (QCP-n, QCP-l, QCP-n-qscd, QCP-l-qscd, QCP-w) and adds requirements to those specified in ETSI TS 119 411-1 [2] to meet Regulation (EU) N° 910/2014. Assessment methods are not covered.

## Definitions and Abbreviations
- **EU Qualified Certificate**: qualified certificate as specified in Regulation (EU) No 910/2014 [i.1]
- **Qualified Electronic Signature/Seal Creation Device (QSCD)**: As specified in Regulation (EU) No 910/2014 [i.1]
- **QCP-n**: Policy for EU qualified certificate issued to a natural person
- **QCP-l**: Policy for EU qualified certificate issued to a legal person
- **QCP-n-qscd**: Policy for EU qualified certificate issued to a natural person where private key resides on a QSCD
- **QCP-l-qscd**: Policy for EU qualified certificate issued to a legal person where private key resides on a QSCD
- **QCP-w**: Policy for EU qualified certificate issued to a web site

## 1 Scope (Detailed)
Specifies policy and security requirements for issuance, maintenance, and life-cycle management of EU qualified certificates for natural persons, legal persons, and web sites. Does not specify assessment methods; Annex B provides a conformity checklist.

### 4.2.5 Certificate policy
Five EU qualified certificate policies defined:
1. **QCP-n**: For natural persons; includes NCP or NCP+ requirements from [2] plus additional EU requirements.
2. **QCP-l**: For legal persons; includes NCP or NCP+ requirements plus additional EU requirements.
3. **QCP-n-qscd**: For natural persons with private key on QSCD; includes NCP+ plus EU requirements.
4. **QCP-l-qscd**: For legal persons with private key on QSCD; includes NCP+ plus EU requirements.
5. **QCP-w**: For web site authentication; includes EVCP plus EU requirements.

Clause 7 allows defining other policies built on these.

## 5 General Provisions on Certification Practice Statement and Certificate Policies
### 5.1 General requirements
Structure follows IETF RFC 3647 [i.5]. Requirements from [2] clause 5.1 **shall** apply.
- `[QCP-n]`, `[QCP-l]`:
  - If no secure cryptographic device required: NCP requirements **shall** apply.
  - If secure cryptographic device required: NCP+ requirements **shall** apply.
- `[QCP-w]`: EVCP requirements **shall** apply.
- `[QCP-n-qscd]`, `[QCP-l-qscd]`: NCP+ requirements **shall** apply.

### 5.2 Certification Practice Statement Requirements
Requirements from [2] clause 5.2 **shall** apply.

### 5.3 Certificate Policy name and identification
Policy identifiers (OIDs) as defined:
- QCP-n: `itu-t(0) identified-organization(4) etsi(0) qualified-certificate-policies(194112) policy-identifiers(1) qcp-natural (0)`
- QCP-l: `... qcp-legal (1)`
- QCP-n-qscd: `... qcp-natural-qscd (2)`
- QCP-l-qscd: `... qcp-legal-qscd (3)`
- QCP-w: `... qcp-web (4)`

Including an identifier indicates the certificate is issued and managed according to this document for that policy.

### 5.5 Certificate Usage
- **QCP-n**: Advanced electronic signatures per Regulation (EU) art. 26, 27.
- **QCP-l**: Advanced electronic seals per Regulation (EU) art. 36, 37.
- **QCP-n-qscd**: Qualified electronic signatures per Regulation (EU) art. 3(12).
- **QCP-l-qscd**: Qualified electronic seals per Regulation (EU) art. 3(27).
- **QCP-w**: Web site authentication per Regulation (EU) art. 45, plus EV certificate purposes.

#### 6.3.5 Key Pair and Certificate Usage
Requirements from [2] clause 6.3.5 **shall** apply. Additional:
- `[QCP-n]`, `[QCP-n-qscd]`, `[QCP-l]`, `[QCP-l-qscd]`: Subject's private key **shall** be maintained under subject's sole control.
- `[QCP-n-qscd]`, `[QCP-l-qscd]`: If subscriber holds QSCD, digital signatures **shall** only be created by such device. If QTSP manages QSCD, private key **shall** not be used for signing except within a QSCD.
- `[QCP-n]`, `[QCP-n-qscd]`, `[QCP-l]`, `[QCP-l-qscd]`: Subject's private key **shall** only be used to create digital signatures.

### 6.5 Technical Security Controls
#### 6.5.1 Key Pair Generation and Installation
Requirements from [2] clause 6.5.1 **shall** apply. Additional:
- `[QCP-n-qscd]`, `[QCP-l-qscd]`: TSP **shall** verify that the device meets appropriate qualification requirements and is certified.

#### 6.5.2 Private Key Protection and Cryptographic Module Engineering Controls
Requirements from [2] clause 6.5.2 **shall** apply. Additional:
- `[QCP-n-qscd]`, `[QCP-l-qscd]`:
  - If device not prepared by TSP, TSP **shall** verify it is a QSCD at registration.
  - If key pair not generated by TSP, request process **shall** ensure public key is from a key pair generated by a QSCD.
  - If key pair managed by another TSP, issuing TSP **shall** verify that TSP is qualified.
- TSP **shall** monitor QSCD certification statuses until end of certificate validity and take appropriate measures; measures **shall** be documented in CPS.

### 6.6 Certificate, CRL, and OCSP Profiles
#### 6.6.1 Certificate Profile
Requirements from [2] clause 6.6.1 **shall** apply. Additional:
- Certificate **shall** be issued per ETSI TS 119 412-5 [4].
- `[QCP-n-qscd]`, `[QCP-l-qscd]`: Certificate **shall** include qcStatement for QSCD (esi4-qcStatement-4).
- `[QCP-n-qscd]`, `[QCP-l-qscd]`: That qcStatement **shall not** be included in other certificates.
- `[QCP-n]`, `[QCP-l]`, `[QCP-n-qscd]`, `[QCP-l-qscd]`, `[QCP-w]`: Certificate **shall** include at least one of: the policy identifier defined in 5.3, or an OID allocated by TSP to the applied certificate policy.
- `[QCP-w]`: Policy identifier from EVCP [3] may be included additionally.
- `[CONDITIONAL]`: If only TSP OID, referred policy **shall** be built according to clause 7 and clearly identify which base policy from this document it adopts.
