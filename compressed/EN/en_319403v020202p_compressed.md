# ETSI EN 319 403 V2.2.2: Electronic Signatures and Infrastructures (ESI); Trust Service Provider Conformity Assessment - Requirements for conformity assessment bodies assessing Trust Service Providers
**Source**: ETSI | **Version**: V2.2.2 | **Date**: 2015-08 | **Type**: Normative (European Standard)
**Original**: REN/ESI-0019403

## Scope (Summary)
This standard specifies requirements for competence, consistent operation, and impartiality of conformity assessment bodies (CABs) assessing and certifying TSPs and their trust services against defined criteria. It applies the general requirements of ISO/IEC 17065 [1] to the specific domain of TSP conformity assessment, independent of trust service type or class.

## Definitions and Abbreviations
- **auditor**: person who assesses conformity to requirements as specified in a given requirements document
- **competence**: ability to apply knowledge and skills to achieve intended results
- **conformity assessment**: process demonstrating whether specified requirements have been fulfilled (from [i.4] and [i.5])
- **conformity assessment body (CAB)**: body that performs conformity assessment services, accredited as competent to assess a qualified TSP and its services (equivalent to [i.4])
- **national accreditation body**: sole body in a State that performs accreditation with authority derived from the State (from [i.4])
- **technical expert**: person who provides specific knowledge or expertise to the audit team, does not act as auditor
- **trust service**: electronic service which enhances trust and confidence in electronic transactions
- **Trust Service Provider (TSP)**: entity which provides one or more electronic trust services

## 4 General Requirements
### 4.1 Legal and Contractual Matters
- **4.1.1-4.1.3**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clauses 4.1.1, 4.1.2, and 4.1.3 respectively.

### 4.2 Management of Impartiality
- **4.2.0**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 4.2. Additionally, TSP-specific requirements:
- **4.2.1**: CABs and their personnel may carry out additional activities not conflicting with impartiality, including:
  - a) organizing/participating in information meetings about certification scheme;
  - b) arranging/participating as lecturer in training courses, provided that when courses relate to TSPs or auditing, lecturers shall confine themselves to generic publicly available information;
  - c) activities prior to audit solely to determine readiness—shall not provide recommendations for specific solutions nor reduce eventual audit duration;
  - d) performing third-party audits according to standards/specifications/regulatory requirements outside accreditation scope;
  - e) adding value during audits (e.g., identifying opportunities for improvement without recommending specific solutions).

### 4.3-4.6 Liability, Financing, Non-discrimination, Confidentiality, Publicly Available Information
- **4.3-4.6**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clauses 4.3, 4.4, 4.5, and 4.6 respectively.

## 5 Structural Requirements
- **5.1**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 5.1 (Organizational structure and top management).
- **5.2**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 5.2 (Mechanism for safeguarding impartiality).

## 6 Resource Requirements
### 6.1 Conformity Assessment Body Personnel
- **6.1.1**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 6.1.1.
- **6.1.2.0**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 6.1.2. Additionally:
- **6.1.2.1**: The following functions (application review, audit, review, certification decision) shall require specific TSP competence as described in clause 6.2.
- **6.1.2.2**: The CAB shall have criteria for training audit teams to demonstrate competence in:
  - a) knowledge of TSP standards and other relevant publicly available specifications;
  - b) understanding of trust services and information security including network security issues;
  - c) understanding of risk assessment and risk management from business perspective;
  - d) technical knowledge of activity to be audited;
  - e) general knowledge of regulatory requirements relevant to TSPs;
  - f) knowledge of security policies and controls.

### 6.2 Resources for Evaluation
- **6.2.0**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 6.2.
- **6.2.1.0**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 6.2.1. Additionally:
- **6.2.1.1**: CAB shall have personnel competent to:
  - a) select and verify competence of TSP auditors for audit teams;
  - b) brief TSP auditors and arrange training;
  - c) decide on granting, maintaining, withdrawing, suspending, extending, or reducing certifications;
  - d) set up and operate appeals and complaints process.
- **6.2.1.2**: CAB personnel shall have knowledge of:
  - a) standards/specifications relevant to TSP conformity assessment;
  - b) TSP general concepts and relevant requirements;
  - c) TSP legal and regulatory requirements;
  - d) trust services functioning, information security including network security;
  - e) TSP security policies and controls;
  - f) TSP risk assessment and risk management.
- **6.2.1.3**: Personnel reviewing TSP applications shall have:
  - a) technological and legal understanding of TSP areas of activity and associated business risks;
  - b) technical understanding of evaluation process;
  - c) understanding of CAB competences and capabilities;
  - d) communication and analytic skills to explain certification requirements and resolve differences.
- **6.2.1.4**: Personnel performing audit (clause 7.4) shall have:
  - **Requirements**:
    1) formal academic qualifications or professional training or extensive experience indicating capability;
    2) at least four years full-time practical workplace experience in IT, of which at least two years in role relating to trust services, PKI, information security, risk assessment/management, network security, or physical security.
  - **Knowledge of**:
    1) audit principles and techniques in TSP audit gained in training course of at least five days;
    2) issues related to trust services, PKI, information security, risk assessment/management, network security, physical security;
    3) applicable standards, specifications, regulatory requirements (e.g., ISO/IEC 27002 [i.13], ETSI EN 319 401 [i.6], ETSI EN 319 411-1 [i.2], ETSI EN 319 411-2 [i.3], ETSI EN 319 421 [i.9], ISO/IEC 15408 [i.7]);
    4) CAB's processes.
  - **Competences**:
    1) language skills appropriate for all organizational levels;
    2) note-taking, report-writing, presentation, interviewing skills;
    3) personal attributes: objective, mature, discerning, analytical, persistent, realistic.
  - **Maintenance**: Competence shall be maintained through education, training, or experience. Prior to assuming auditor role, candidate shall have gained experience in entire TSP audit process by participating under supervision in at least four TSP audits totaling at least 20 days, including documentation review, on-site audit, and audit reporting.
- **6.2.1.5**: Personnel performing review shall have competences per 6.2.1.4 and have acted as auditor in at least three complete TSP assessments.
- **6.2.1.6**: Personnel performing certification decision shall have competences per 6.2.1.2 and knowledge of certification process.
- **6.2.1.7**: Technical experts (not auditors) shall be responsible to the audit team leader. They provide specific knowledge (e.g., legislative/regulatory, technical state-of-art, risk assessment, network security, product evaluation). They do not need complete auditor competences.
- **6.2.1.8**: Audit teams shall be competent as a whole. At least one auditor in the team shall satisfy criteria for:
  - a) managing the team (lead auditor);
  - b) demonstrated knowledge of legislative/regulatory requirements and legal compliance in TSP and information security;
  - c) demonstrated knowledge of current technical state-of-art regarding TSP and PKI;
  - d) demonstrated knowledge in technologies applicable to the audited trust service;
  - e) demonstrated knowledge of performing information security risk assessments to identify assets, threats, vulnerabilities, impact, and mitigation;
  - f) demonstrated knowledge of organizational reliability issues.
  - Team should be competent to trace security incident indications back to appropriate controls. One-person team allowed if all criteria met.
- **6.2.1.9**: Audit team leaders shall, in addition to 6.2.1.4, have:
  - a) acted as auditor in at least three complete TSP audits;
  - b) adequate knowledge and attributes to manage audit process;
  - c) competence to communicate effectively orally and in writing.

## 7 Process Requirements
### 7.1 General
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.1. Evaluation shall take form of audit against defined criteria that should:
  - a) take into account specificities of trust service type;
  - b) ensure all aspects of TSP activity are fully covered;
  - c) be based on standards, specifications, or regulatory requirements (e.g., ETSI EN 319 401 [i.6], ETSI EN 319 411-1 [i.2], ETSI EN 319 411-2 [i.3], ETSI EN 319 421 [i.9], Regulation (EU) No 910/2014 [i.1]).

### 7.2 Application
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.2.

### 7.3 Application Review
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.3. Additionally, CAB shall ensure it does not accept application for which it is not competent. CAB shall review contract based on competence analysis, demonstrating ability to:
  - a) understand TSP areas of activity and business risks;
  - b) define competencies needed to certify, considering TSP activities, trust services, and security risks;
  - c) confirm availability of required competences.

### 7.4 Audit
- **7.4.0**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.4. Additional requirements follow.
- **7.4.1 Audit Scope**
  - **7.4.1.0**: CAB documentation shall include policies and procedures for audit process, including checklists and procedures for assessing conformity. Audit team shall audit TSP and trust services against all applicable requirements. CAB shall ensure scope and boundaries are clearly defined (business characteristics, organization, facilities, assets, technology). CAB shall ensure TSP's risk assessment properly reflects activities and extends to boundaries, including interfaces with services/activities not completely within scope (e.g., shared facilities).
  - **7.4.1.1 Audit Team Mandate**: Mandate shall be clearly defined and made known to TSP. It shall require examining structure, policies, procedures, practices, management, and operation to confirm all requirements are met and procedures are implemented. Technical experts may complement team but cannot replace auditors. CAB shall have procedure for selecting auditors/experts based on competence, training, qualifications, experience, and for monitoring auditor performance.
  - **7.4.1.2 Audit Methodology**: Plan and date shall be agreed with TSP. Reporting procedures shall ensure:
    - a) closing meeting before leaving premises: written/oral indication of conformity and opportunity for TSP questions;
    - b) audit team leader provides report of findings to CAB.
  - CAB shall have procedures to verify if TSP has prior audit programme or certifications for different sites. Audit procedures shall not presuppose particular implementation or documentation format. They shall focus on establishing whether requirements are met. If network-assisted auditing techniques used, TSP shall be informed of security implications; audit plan shall identify them.
- **7.4.2 Audit Time**: CAB shall allow sufficient time considering: size and complexity of trust service, type of business, technology diversity, number of sites, previous performance, outsourcing, applicable standards, existing certifications. Justification of time shall be documented.
- **7.4.3 Multiple Sites**
  - **7.4.3.1**: Sample-based approach may be considered if TSP security management scheme meets: a) security administered under control of TSP’s security policy administration; b) all sites subject to TSP's management review programme. Applicable sites are those directly concerned with operations meeting TSP policy requirements.
  - **7.4.3.2**: When using sampling, CAB shall ensure:
    - a) initial contract review identifies differences between sites;
    - b) representative number sampled considering internal audit results, management review, site size/business variation, complexity, working practices, activities, interaction with critical systems, subcontracting, differing regulatory requirements;
    - c) sample partly selective based on above factors and partly non-selective;
    - d) every site subject to significant threats/assets/vulnerabilities included;
    - e) surveillance programme within reasonable time covers all sites unless no impact;
    - f) nonconformity at head office or single site requires corrective action applied to head office and all impacted sites.
  - Audit shall address central site activities to ensure central security administration is applied to all sites. CAB shall justify number of sites audited.
- **7.4.4 Audit Report**
  - **7.4.4.1**: Report to TSP and other legally entitled parties shall contain:
    - a) account of audit including summary of document review and criteria;
    - b) account of audit of TSP's information security risk analysis;
    - c) total audit time and detailed specification per phase;
    - d) audit trails followed, rationale for selection, methodology including sampling and test procedures.
  - **7.4.4.2**: Report of findings to CAB shall be sufficiently detailed to support certification decision and contain:
    - a) areas covered, sites audited, audit trails, methodologies;
    - b) positive and negative observations;
    - c) details of nonconformities with objective evidence and reference to criteria;
    - d) comments on conformity with clear statement of nonconformity and comparison with previous audits if applicable.
  - Completed questionnaires, checklists, observations, logs, auditor notes may form integral part and shall be submitted as evidence. Information about samples evaluated should be included. Report shall consider adequacy of internal organization and procedures.
- **7.4.5 Audit Process**
  - **7.4.5.1 General preparation for initial audit**: CAB shall require TSP to make arrangements for documentation and access to all areas, records, and personnel. Applicant shall provide: general information about trust service; locations, sizes, functions of TSP and subcontractor sites; copy of documentation on policies and practices, IT network infrastructure plans, manuals, instructions.
  - **7.4.5.2 Audit Process**: Objective: confirm and certify compliance with applicable assessment criteria. Auditors shall review what records are confidential/sensitive; if effective audit not possible, CAB shall inform TSP that audit can proceed only after appropriate access arrangements. Audit includes visits to TSP sites (see multi-site clause 7.4.3). CAB shall agree when and where audit is conducted. Audit shall be performed in at least two stages.
  - **7.4.5.3 Stage 1 audit**: Focus on obtaining and reviewing documentation on TSP and audited service(s) (including design documentation). Objectives: provide focus for stage 2 by understanding structure and extent. Includes document review and verification of records (legal entity, liability coverage, contractual relationships, internal/external audits, management review, partial compliances). Stage 1 report shall contain: organizational structure including subcontractors; document review summary; account of audit of risk analysis; assessment of stage 2 likelihood and need for additional resources; audit time spent; areas of concern; methodology. Document review shall be completed before stage 2. Results documented in written report with recommendations; findings communicated to client. Interval between stage 1 and 2 shall consider client's need to resolve concerns.
  - **7.4.5.4 Stage 2 audit**: Shall always take place at TSP site(s). Based on stage 1 observations, auditors draft audit plan. Objectives: a) confirm TSP adheres to its policies, objectives, procedures; b) confirm implemented trust services conform to applicable criteria. Focus on evidence regarding: implementation of trust service requirements; organizational and technical processes; implemented information security measures including network protection; products (trustworthy systems) such as cryptographic modules; physical security of relevant sites.
- **7.4.6 Audit Frequency**: Period of no greater than two years for full (re-)assessment audit unless otherwise required by applicable legislation or commercial scheme. (Surveillance audits may be required at any time as per clause 7.9.)

### 7.5 Review
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.5.

### 7.6 Certification Decision
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.6. Additionally, decision can be:
  - a) certified: audited trust service fulfils criteria;
  - b) passed with pending nonconformities: conditional on corrective actions within 3 months if nonconformities do not impact ability to meet intended service;
  - c) not certified.

### 7.7 Certification Documentation
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.7. Additionally, scope shall identify relevant trust service policy or policies. CAB shall provide formal documentation that conveys or permits identification of national accreditation body.

### 7.8 Directory of Certified Products
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.8. Additionally, CAB shall maintain and make publicly accessible up-to-date information on certified TSPs and trust services.

### 7.9 Surveillance
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.9. Additionally, CAB shall define periodic surveillance and re-assessment programme including on-site audits. At least one surveillance audit per year recommended. Surveillance audit shall include:
  - a) review of actions on previous nonconformities;
  - b) review of multi-site sampling strategy if applied;
  - c) review of changes in documentation and operation;
  - d) review of internal audits and management review;
  - e) treatment of complaints;
  - f) use of marks/references;
  - g) review of public statements.
  - Surveillance need not be full system audits; shall be planned with other activities and consider multi-site sampling. Historical period records sample shall be examined. Reports shall contain audit information on clearing of nonconformities.

### 7.10 Changes Affecting Certification
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clause 7.10. Additionally, changes initiated by client include but are not limited to: major changes in documentation; changes in policies, objectives, procedures affecting trust service; security relevant changes (e.g., trustworthy systems, network security, physical infrastructure, new sites). CAB shall establish procedure for notification and determination of appropriate activities; notification and decision before implementation. Full re-assessment should occur for: major changes to scope; major changes to trust services; new trust service; major changes to IT systems or business processes; major relocation.

### 7.11-7.13 Termination, Reduction, Suspension, Withdrawal; Records; Complaints and Appeals
- Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clauses 7.11, 7.12, and 7.13 respectively.

## 8 Management System Requirements
- **8.1-8.8**: Shall comply with ISO/IEC 17065 [1] clauses 8.1 (Options), 8.2 (General management system documentation), 8.3 (Control of documents), 8.4 (Control of records), 8.5 (Management review), 8.6 (Internal audits), 8.7 (Corrective actions), and 8.8 (Preventive actions) respectively.
