# ETSI TR 101 533-2 V1.3.1: Electronic Signatures and Infrastructures (ESI); Data Preservation Systems Security; Part 2: Guidelines for Assessors
**Source**: ETSI Technical Committee ESI | **Version**: V1.3.1 | **Date**: 2012-04 | **Type**: Informative Technical Report  
**Original**: http://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_tr/101500_101599/10153302/01.03.01_60/tr_10153302v010301p.pdf

## Scope (Summary)
This document provides guidelines for Assessors reviewing and auditing the Information Security Management System (ISMS) of a Data Preservation System (DPS). It does not address Assessor qualification (see ISO/IEC 17021, ISO/IEC 27006) nor basic audit activities. Recommendations are based on ISO/IEC 27001, ISO/IEC 27002, TS 102 573, and TS 101 533-1, enhancing them where necessary.

## Normative References
*Not applicable.*

## Definitions and Abbreviations
Terms and abbreviations are given in TS 101 533-1 [i.4].

## 4 Overview
- Assessors should use this document to assess compliance of a DPSP with TS 101 533-1 [i.4].
- Assessors may skip ISO/IEC 27002 controls if the DPSP demonstrates a valid ISO/IEC 27001 audit covering the same SoA.
- Assessors must verify the legal system(s) the DPSP operates in and have necessary legal competence.

### 4.1 Preservation Service types
- **Core Data Preservation Service**: shall be implemented by each DPSP.
- **Extended Services**: may be provided; marked with [EXT].
- Assessors should verify compliance with core and implemented extended services.

## 5 Provisions based on TS 102 573

### 5.1 Compliance with TS 101 533-1
1. Assessors should assess DPSP against all controls declared applicable in the SoA.
2. Verify formal declaration of compliance in ISPD, procedures, job descriptions, subcontractor agreements.
3. Review previous assessment deficiencies and ensure correction.

### 5.2 DPSP Obligations specified in TS 102 573, clause 6

#### 5.2.1 Arrangements to cover liabilities and financial stability
- Assessors should verify that Risk Assessment includes financial risks (ref. TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.1).

#### 5.2.2 Conformance by Subcontractors
1. Assessors should identify themselves at DPSP and external provider premises after prior notification.
2. Assess agreements between DPSP and subcontractors shall include compliance with security measures.
3. Agreements shall specify DPSP's right to perform inspections (periodical and impromptu) on subcontractor premises; ascertain existence of previous inspection reports.
4. Assess that subcontractors comply with TS 101 533-1 [i.4].

#### 5.2.3 DPSP service provisions in abidance by the applicable legislation
1. Verify existence of documentation showing DPSP is a legal entity.
2. Review contractual documentation that specifies legal requirements for each service.
3. Ascertain contractual documents are written as per TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.3 item 5).
4. Verify existence of procedures for identification and authentication of legitimately entitled persons (ref. TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.3 item 6).
5. **[EXT.1] Personal data preservation**: If metadata created from personal data, review contractual agreements addressing issues in TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.3 item 7; verify metadata managed as private data.

#### 5.2.4 Contractual aspects
1. Review agreements addressing DPSP duties towards subscribers (ref. TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.4).
2. Assessors are advised to seek legal assistance for legal validity of agreements.
3. Verify existence and allocation of resources for DPSP obligations per clauses 5.2.4 and 5.2.1 of TS 101 533-1.
4. Verify DPSP can correctly display preserved data objects per contractual agreements (sample-based).

#### 5.2.5 Resolution of complaints and disputes
1. Gather evidence that deposit-related procedures comply with service agreement and record information per TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.5 items 1) and 2).
2. **[EXT.1] Personal Data Preservation**: Verify procedures for compliance with TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.5 item 3) and check effectiveness.

#### 5.2.6 Organisation independence
- Gather evidence of DPSP’s independence from customers/providers and that decisions are free from undue influence.

#### 5.2.7 DPSP Subscriber Obligations
1. Verify TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.7 provisions are met, especially agreements on identification/authentication methods of persons entitled to act for subscriber.
2. Verify procedures ensuring subscribers provide and regularly update information per TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.7 item 2).
3. Verify existence of statements per TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.7 item 3) in subscriber agreement.

#### 5.2.8 Information for trading partners
1. Verify terms and conditions for trading partners per TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.8 are implemented.
2. Ascertain existence of evidence (if applicable) that subscribers are informed of DPSP’s recognition status by an external body and the body’s official status.

#### 5.2.9 Information for auditor/regulatory/tax authorities
- Verify documented procedures per TS 101 533-1 clause 5.2.9 are in place and implemented.

## 6 Objectives and controls in TS 102 573, annex A

### 6.1 SS.1. Signature

#### 6.1.1 SS.1.1. Class of Electronic Signature
- Ascertain that one mechanism required by TS 101 533-1 is in place. If based on electronic signature issued on behalf of DPSP, it shall be at least AdES type in compliance with applicable legislation.

#### 6.1.2 SS.1.2. Certification
1. Assess compliance with TS 101 533-1 clause 6.1.2.
2. If QES used, review that the CA was recognised as valid by relevant authority at time of certificate issuance.
3. Verify signature certificates meet supported signature requirements (e.g., limitations of use).

#### 6.1.3 SS.1.3. Signature Creation Data
1. If SSCD used, review they are provided and used in compliance with applicable CP/CPS.
2. Review that DPSP activation data management procedures are consistent with CA specifications.
3. If SSCD not used, gather evidence of reliable procedures ensuring SCD confidentiality.

#### 6.1.4 SS.1.4. Certificate Subject's Registration
- Gather evidence that DS certificates are issued upon specific agreements with CAs covering TS 101 533-1 clause 6.1.4. Verify that Delegate Signers are officially appointed.

#### 6.1.5 SS.1.5. Certificate Revocation
1. Ascertain that certificate management agreement specifies who (apart from subject) is entitled to request revocation.
2. Ascertain existence of auditable procedure for requesting revocation, consistent with TS 101 533-1 clause 6.1.5, and verify its implementation.
3. If revocation procedure has been enacted, verify audit trail makes it auditable.

### 6.2 SS.2. Maintenance of Signature over Storage Period
- Ascertain existence and abidance by a procedure ensuring signatures applied by DPSP (e.g., on Closure Evidence) are maintained per TS 101 533-1 clause 6.2.

### 6.3 SS.3. Storage

#### 6.3.1 SS.3.1. Authorized Access (see also clause 6.4)
1. Gather evidence of procedures for person authentication/registration; DPSP shall demonstrate compliance.
2. Gather evidence of procedures governing modification/deletion of preserved data objects; DPSP shall demonstrate compliance.
3. If end-to-end encryption is used, verify existence of agreement on encryption system with counterpart.
4. Ascertain segregation among subscribers is ensured.

#### 6.3.2 SS.2. Authenticity and Integrity
1. Gather evidence of procedure to detect loss or surreptitious modification/addition of data objects per TS 101 533-1 clause 6.3.2, and correct implementation.
2. Ascertain provisions on Closure Evidence usage (including algorithm weakening) or alternate mechanisms are complied with.
3. Check existence of exhaustive audit trail generated by procedures.

#### 6.3.3 SS.3.3. Data Object Readability **[EXT1]**
1. Ascertain that:
   a) If TS 101 533-1 clause 6.3.3 item 1)a) applies: all required software/hardware/equipment are reliably and securely kept.
   b) If item 1)b) applies: formats meet specified requirements; where item 1)b) ii) applies, assertion by trusted third party exists that transposed data objects maintain original semantics.
2. Verify procedures per TS 101 533-1 clause 6.3.3 item 2) are performed on schedule.
3. If degradation identified, verify DPSP properly and timely managed event as security event and performed data object recreation.
4. Verify BCP encompasses TS 101 533-1 clause 6.3.3 item 1), particularly 1)a).

#### 6.3.4 SS.3.4. Storage media type
1. If DPSP agreed on specific media types with subscribers, verify those media are actually used.
2. Gather evidence:
   a) DPSP has procedures to be timely informed when media types do not last for preservation period.
   b) DPSP has procedures to timely migrate to another media type and perform data object recreation, and has implemented them if event occurred.

#### 6.3.5 SS.3.5. Data Objects Format
1. Ascertain that agreements specify if submitted data objects are analog and/or electronic.
2. Verify compliance with TS 101 533-1 clause 6.3.5 according to Extended Services provided.

#### 6.3.6 SS.3.6. Requirements on Separation and Confidentiality
- Verify compliance with TS 102 573 annex A SS.3.6 through existence of auditable procedures and technical/organisational measures.

### 6.4 SS.4. Reporting to and Exchanges with Authorities
1. Ascertain existence of procedures for remote data object access ensuring confidentiality per applicable law, and compliance.
2. Ascertain compliance with TS 102 573 annex A SS.4.2 and clause 4.3.

### 6.5 SS.5. Conversion of Analog Originals to Digital Formats **[EXT1]**
1. Ascertain that conversion procedures are defined based on Risk Assessment outcomes.
2. Verify existence of procedures specified in TS 101 533-1 clause 6.5 item 1) and that they produce auditable trail.
3. Where TS 101 533-1 clause 6.5 item 2) applies, ascertain compliance.

## Annex A: ISO/IEC 27001 related Long Term Preservation-specific ISMS guidelines for control assessment

### A.5 Security Policy

#### A.5.1 Information security policy

##### A.5.1.1 Information security policy document
- Assessors should be provided with DPSP's ISPD.
- Ascertain that ISPD addresses preserved data objects classification levels.
- Verify audit trails show ISPD distributed to all interested persons.

##### A.5.1.2 Review of the information security policy
- Verify ISPD is reviewed per TS 101 533-1 clause A.5.1.2 item 1).
- Verify existence of records showing reviewed ISPD enforced consistently with Risk Assessment timeliness.

### A.6 Organization of information security

#### A.6.1 Internal organization

##### A.6.1.1 Management commitment to information security
- Verify existence of a complete registration of persons Responsible for Preservation.
- Verify all depositors are made aware of up-to-date DPSP organisation as it affects them.
- Verify existence of records on education programme provided to DPSP persons.

##### A.6.1.2 Information security co-ordination
- Ascertain that from DPSP organisation chart, security is governed to prevent conflicting practices (e.g., hierarchically).

##### A.6.1.3 Allocation of information security responsibilities
- Ascertain that documentation per TS 101 533-1 clause A.6.1.3 2) exists and is up to date.

##### A.6.1.4 Authorization process for information processing facilities
- Ascertain that procedures ensure new technology is submitted to Information Security management for approval before implementation.
- Ascertain that for each new technology implemented there is prior consent.

##### A.6.1.5 Confidentiality agreements
- Ascertain existence of procedure requiring that all formal communications to outside are signed off by relevant Management.
- Ascertain existence of procedures forbidding employees to reveal confidential information without written derogation from data objects owner; verify such derogations.

##### A.6.1.6 Contact with authorities
- Ascertain that organisation chart clearly specifies names/job titles of officers entitled to speak with authorities.

##### A.6.1.7 Contact with special interest groups
- Ascertain that DPSP provides evidence of capability to keep up to date with information.

##### A.6.1.8 Independent review of information security
- Ascertain that if previous assessments were by accredited auditors, DPSP can exhibit evidence of verifying their accreditation.

#### A.6.2 External Parties

##### A.6.2.1 Identification of risks related to external parties
- Ascertain that Risk Assessment addresses risks related to external parties.

##### A.6.2.2 Addressing security when dealing with customers
- Act consistently with ISO/IEC 27001 based auditing.

##### A.6.2.3 Addressing security in third party agreements
1. Ascertain existence of guidelines (possibly checklist) for topics in agreements with third parties, consistent with Risk Assessment.
2. Inspect some existing agreements to verify they meet guidelines per TS 101 533-1 clause A.6.2.3.
3. Verify that for each outsourced activity, relevant management has formally accepted provider's ISPD, positively evaluated by Assessors without conflict.
4. Verify agreements require service continuity meeting DPSP BCP requirements.
5. Verify that for each outsourced service, a back-out plan exists, regularly assessed and approved.
6. Verify agreements explicitly provide DPSP right to perform inspections at providers.
7. If DPSP operates ad hoc developed SW, verify escrow facility exists for most recent version of SW code and documentation.

### A.7 Asset Management

#### A.7.1 Responsibility for assets

##### A.7.1.1 Inventory of assets

##### A.7.1.2 Ownership of assets
- Ascertain existence of information on appointment per TS 101 533-1 clause A.7.1.2.
- Verify that "Disaster Recovery Team" authorisations are activated only upon invocation of Disaster (real or drill).

##### A.7.1.3 Acceptable use of assets
- Act consistently with ISO/IEC 27001 based auditing.

#### A.7.2 Information classification

##### A.7.2.1 Classification guidelines
1. Ascertain existence of auditable classification mechanism.
2. Ascertain classifications assigned by data objects owners are implemented.
3. Ascertain contractual documentation specifies consequences of lack of classification indication.
4. Ascertain confidentiality classification is reviewed per TS 101 533-1 clause A.7.2.1.

##### A.7.2.2 Information labelling and handling
- Ascertain compliance with TS 101 533-1 clause A.7.2.2.

- Ascertain existence of provisions in agreements stating DPSP's right to perform regular and extemporaneous inspections.

##### A.10.2.3 Managing changes to third party services
- Ascertain existence of auditable procedures on DPSP and outsourcers recording change process per TS 101 533-1 clause A.10.2.3.

##### A.10.4.1 Controls against malicious code

##### A.10.4.2 Controls against mobile code
- Act consistently with ISO/IEC 27001 based auditing.

#### A.10.6 Network security management

##### A.10.6.1 Network controls
- Act consistently with ISO/IEC 27001 based auditing.

##### A.10.6.2 Security of network services
- Ascertain that networks architecture (including Firewalls and Intrusion Detection Systems) meets requirements in TS 101 533-1 clause A.10.6.2.

#### A.10.7 Media handling

##### A.10.7.1 Management of removable media

##### A.10.8.2 Exchange agreements

#### A.10.10 Monitoring

##### A.10.10.1 Audit logging
- Ascertain that for all DPS computer systems, technical procedures ensure logs addressing information specified in clause of reference are created.

- Act consistently with ISO/IEC 27001 based auditing.

### A.11 Access control

2. Ascertain existence of procedures ensuring consistency between input and output data objects for every acceptance phase.
3. Ascertain that records provide evidence that integrity assurance mechanism agreed with customers is regularly performed.
4. **[EXT1] Input data object signature verification**: Verify that agreement between DPSP and customer complies with TS 101 533-1 clause A.12.2.1, via signature verification process log and subsequent process steps.

##### A.12.2.2 Control of internal processing
1. Ascertain that for each depositor and, possibly, for each data object type, specific officers are specified per TS 101 533-1 clause A.12.2.2.
2. Ascertain that log records exist for procedures ensuring no data object dropped or modified in each step of preservation process.

##### A.12.2.3 Message integrity
- Act consistently with ISO/IEC 27001 based auditing.

##### A.12.2.4 Output data validation
- Act consistently with ISO/IEC 27001 based auditing.

#### A.12.3 Cryptographic controls

##### A.12.3.1 Policy on the use of cryptographic controls
1. Verify existence of formal documentation showing cryptographic choices are based on de jure or de facto technical standards, and deviations are consistent with exceptions in TS 101 533-1.
2. **[EXT1] Preserving encrypted data objects**: For conditional services per TS 101 533-1 clause A.12.3.1, verify:
   a) Data object agreed or legally required to be encrypted is not preserved in clear.
   b) Dual control based procedures exist to decrypt upon request by authorized entity.

##### A.12.3.2 Key management
1. Ascertain that records exist for any key management related process.
2. Where encryption algorithms used, verify existence of emergency procedures to timely deal with situations per TS 101 533-1 clause A.12.3.2. Ascertain that evidence exists that these procedures implement Dual Control. If Dual Control not used, ascertain that formal documentation provides rationale.

### A.13 Information security incident management

#### A.13.1 Reporting Information Security Events and Weaknesses

##### A.13.1.1 Reporting information security events
1. Ascertain that security events reporting is governed by formal procedure applicable to DPSP officers and subcontractors, ensuring timely and correct reporting with severity level.
2. Verify existence of records showing all DPSP officers and subcontractors have declared in writing they are aware of incident reporting procedures and consequences of non-abidance.

##### A.13.1.2 Reporting security weaknesses
1. Ascertain that security weaknesses reporting is governed by formal procedure applicable to DPSP officers and subcontractors, ensuring timely and correct reporting with severity level.
2. Verify existence of records showing all DPSP officers and subcontractors have declared in writing they are aware of weaknesses reporting procedures and consequences of non-abidance.

#### A.13.2 Management of Information Security Incidents and Improvements

##### A.13.2.1 Responsibilities and procedures

2. Ascertain that records show all required communications to involved persons have been enacted and obligations formally accepted.
3. Verify correct and complete documentation of emergency cases handling.

##### A.13.2.2 Learning from information security incidents

2. Verify that it is clearly indicated upon occurrence of what minimum security event severity level the revision of Risk Assessment and/or ISPD is to be performed extemporaneously.

##### A.13.2.3 Collection of evidence
- Act consistently with ISO/IEC 27001 based auditing.

### A.14 Business continuity management

#### A.14.1 Information security aspects of business continuity management

##### A.14.1.1 Including information security in the business continuity management process
1. Ascertain that DPSP BCP is designed to restart operations within precise time periods, assessed against agreements and applicable legislation, and that records of BCP implementation procedure simulations proved to meet such limits.
2. Verify that tests have been made to verify that time periods can be met or have effectively been met.

##### A.14.1.2 Business continuity and risk assessment
1. Ascertain that DPSP BCP has been drafted giving highest priority to personnel safety, according to commonly accepted specifications, taking into account Service Discontinuity Impact Analysis on DPS components with criticality level scale.
2. Verify that DPS components have been assigned a scale of criticality level or that DPSP has provided exhaustive explanation in SoA.
3. Verify that BCP specifies maximum downtime acceptable at least for services and processes indicated in TS 101 533-1 clause A.14.1.2.

##### A.14.1.3 Developing and implementing continuity plans including information security
- Ascertain that an assessment has been performed to identify "acceptable loss of information and services" and that outcomes are correctly reported in subscriber agreements.

##### A.14.1.4 Business continuity planning framework
1. Ascertain that DPSP documentation clearly specifies the person (by name or role) in charge of invoking the BCP.
2. Verify existence of Disaster Recovery Team with formally appointed components and rationale for appointment. If team members are not DPSP employees, documentation should show they abide by TS 101 533-1 clause A.14.1.4.
3. If disaster recovery/back-up site is under control of a Service Provider, verify existence of reliability assessment and agreement addressing BCP requirements, including adequate sanctions for default.

##### A.14.1.5 Testing, maintaining and re-assessing business continuity plans
1. Ascertain that DPSP has testing plans for at least ISO/IEC 27002 items specified in TS 101 533-1 clause A.14.1.5.
2. Verify existence of records showing rehearsals are planned and performed according to schedule, with participation of external service providers.
3. Verify agreements with DPSP service providers address obligations in TS 101 533-1 clause A.14.1.5.
4. Verify existence of records showing DPSP performed inspections on its service providers as per that clause.
