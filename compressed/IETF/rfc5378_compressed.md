# RFC 5378: Rights Contributors Provide to the IETF Trust
**Source**: IETF (Best Current Practice 78) | **Version**: Obsoletes RFC 3978, 4748; Updates RFC 2026 | **Date**: November 2008 | **Type**: Normative (Sections 1, 2, 5, 6) / Informative (others)
**Original**: https://www.rfc-editor.org/info/rfc5378

## Scope (Summary)
This document defines IETF policies on rights in Contributions to ensure they can be used within the IETF Standards Process while allowing contributors to retain as many rights as possible. It establishes that contributors grant a perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive, royalty-free, world-wide, sublicensable license to the IETF Trust for publication, translation, derivative works, and trademark use, subject to certain limitations. It supersedes RFC 3978 and RFC 4748.

## Definitions and Abbreviations
- **Contribution**: Any submission to the IETF intended for publication as an Internet-Draft or RFC, and any statement made within an IETF activity (including oral statements, emails to lists, etc.). Excludes "RFC Editor Contributions" as defined in Section 4.
- **Contributor**: An individual submitting a Contribution.
- **Indirect Contributor**: Any person who has materially or substantially contributed without personally submitting.
- **Copyright**: All exclusive rights an author has in a work (copy, publish, distribute, create derivative works). Not limited to "right to copy".
- **IETF**: Includes all individuals participating in IETF-organized activities.
- **IETF Documents**: RFCs and Internet-Drafts used in the IETF Standards Process (IETF stream).
- **IETF Standards Process**: Activities undertaken by the IETF in settings described in the Contribution definition.
- **IETF Trust**: Trust established under Virginia, USA law to hold and administer IP rights for the IETF.
- **Internet-Draft**: Temporary documents with a maximum lifetime of six months in the IETF Secretariat's public directory.
- **Legend Instructions**: Standardized text and instructions maintained by the IETF Trust for inclusion in IETF Documents (posted at http://trustee.ietf.org/license-info).
- **RFC**: The publication series used by the IETF; unchanged after publication.
- **Reasonably and personally known**: Knowledge an individual personally has or would reasonably be expected to have due to their job.
- **Non-IETF documents**: Internet-Drafts submitted to the RFC Editor independently of the IETF Standards Process.

## 2. Introduction
**Normative sections**: 1, 2, 5, and 6 are normative; others are informative.

### 2.1. No Retroactive Effect
This memo does not retroactively obtain rights from Contributions preceding the date the IETF Trust announces adoption of these procedures.

## 3. Exposition (Informative – Condensed)
Explains rationale: contributions must grant non-exclusive rights for development and publication; contributors retain all other rights. The IETF Trust holds IP and grants sublicenses to participants. Derivative works rights are needed for evolution of standards; exceptions exist for proprietary technologies or republications of other standards. Trademark licenses are limited to use in exercising other rights. For works not subject to copyright, contributors must still grant equivalent rights.

## 4. Non-IETF Documents
This document applies only to IETF stream Contributions. Other streams (IRTF, Independent, etc.) are not covered unless the controlling entity adopts these rules. Non-IETF Contributions must be marked per Legend Instructions.

## 5. Rights in Contributions (Normative)

### 5.1. General Policy
- **Shall**: By submission, each Contributor is deemed to read, understand, and enter into a legally-binding agreement to comply with this document.
- **Shall**: Contributor must obtain necessary permissions from any party reasonably and personally known to have rights (including employer/sponsor).
- **Shall**: No further acknowledgment or signature is required.

### 5.2. Confidentiality Obligations
- **Shall not**: No information subject to confidentiality or restriction on dissemination may be submitted as a Contribution.
- **Shall**: Any statement implying confidentiality is disregarded and has no force.

### 5.3. Rights Granted by Contributors to the IETF Trust
- **Shall grant**: Perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive, royalty-free, world-wide, sublicensable license to IETF Trust under all copyrights and other rights in the Contribution:
  - a. to copy, publish, display, and distribute the Contribution in whole or in part;
  - b. to prepare translations into languages other than English and distribute them;
  - c. to modify or prepare derivative works (other than translations) unless explicitly disallowed in notices per Legend Instructions;
  - d. to reproduce trademarks, service marks, trade names solely in connection with the above, preserving TM/R identifiers.

### 5.4. Sublicenses by the IETF Trust
- **Shall**: IETF Trust will sublicense rights under Section 5.3 to IETF participants for use within the IETF Standards Process (per license at http://trustee.ietf.org/license-info).
- **May**: IETF Trust may grant additional sublicenses, guided by RFC 5377.

### 5.5. No Patent License
- **Shall not**: Licenses in Section 5.3 grant no right under any patent or similar IP right.

### 5.6. Representations and Warranties
- **Shall represent**: Each Contributor represents to the best of knowledge/ability:
  - a. Contribution properly acknowledges all Contributors (including Indirect Contributors).
  - b. No information is confidential; IETF and affiliates may freely disclose.
  - c. No limits to ability to make grants, acknowledgments, agreements that are reasonably and personally known.
  - d. Not intentionally included defamatory, untrue, or illegal material.
  - e. All trademarks, trade names, service marks, proprietary names reasonably and personally known are clearly designated.

### 5.7. No Duty to Publish
- **Shall acknowledge**: IETF has no duty to publish or use any Contribution; may withdraw non-compliant Contributions.

### 5.8. Trademarks
- **Shall**: Contributors claiming trademark rights are requested to state conditions for implementers (per RFC 3979 Section 6).

### 5.9. Copyright in RFCs
- **Shall**: Contributor acknowledges copyright in any RFC (other than RFC Editor Contributions) is owned by IETF Trust.
- **Shall**: Contributor is deemed to assign copyright interest in the collective work upon submission for publication.

### 5.10. Contributors' Retention of Rights
- **Shall**: IETF Trust issues license confirming Contributors may use their Contributions as published in RFC in any way, subject only to not representing as RFC unless full/complete copy/translation.

## 6. Legends, Notices and Other Standardized Text in IETF Documents (Normative)
- **Shall**: Certain standardized text (per Legend Instructions) must be reproduced verbatim in IETF Documents (mandatory copyright notices; optional derivative work limitations).
- **Shall**: The version of text in IETF Documents is that posted in Legend Instructions on the date of publication.
- **Shall**: IETF may refuse to publish Contributions lacking required legends/notices.
- **Shall not**: Additional copyright notices are not permitted except for joint development or republication from other standards organizations (requires IAB approval).

## 7. Security Considerations (Informative)
No known security issues related to IETF Contribution rights policies.

## Informative Annexes (Condensed)
- **Section 3 (Exposition)**: Explains rationale for requiring non-exclusive rights, derivative works, and trademark licenses. Highlights that rights are granted to the IETF Trust, which sublicenses to participants. Exceptions for proprietary technologies and third-party standards.
- **Section 4 (Non-IETF Documents)**: Clarifies that this document applies only to the IETF stream; other streams (IRTF, Independent) are not covered unless adopted.
- **Section 7 (Security Considerations)**: No security implications for these policies.
- **Section 9 (Acknowledgments)**: Thanks to IETF IPR Working Group.
- **Section 10 (Changes since RFC 3978)**: Major reorganization; limited to rights granted to IETF Trust; sublicensing delegated to Trust; definitions updated; binding contract clarified; derivative works not limited to IETF; copyright ownership clarified.
- **Section 11 (IAB Declaration)**: IAB stream is covered by this document; other streams require separate approval per RFC 4844.
