🛸 One of the more interesting conversations in RegTech/GRC is not just about monitoring change. It is about understanding obligation with precision and context.

In a recent strategy call with (
MCO (MyComplianceOffice)
, one area that stood out was KYO — Know Your Obligations. That may sound straightforward, but in practice it is one of the most difficult and strategically important challenges organizations face. And it is one of the strategy engagements I have in the Netherlands next week with a global bank. 

⚠️ Too many compliance programs are still navigating the galaxy with partial star charts. They can see regulations, track alerts, and manage workflows, but they still struggle to clearly identify what obligations apply, to whom they apply, where they apply, and what must be done about them. That is where the mission often breaks down.

🔭 What makes the KYO conversation important is that it shifts the focus from raw regulatory volume to relevant obligation intelligence. It is not enough to collect the universe of regulatory change. Organizations need to map the parts of that universe that actually matter to their business model, jurisdictions, products, services, controls, and operational reality.

🧭 This is where the market is headed. The future is not simply more content. The future is better contextualization, better traceability, and better alignment between regulatory requirements, policies, controls, and business operations.

🤖 From an analyst perspective, this is also where RegTech starts to become more strategic. When a solution helps an organization know its obligations with clarity, it is no longer just a compliance tracking tool. It becomes part of the organization’s navigation system for risk, resilience, and integrity.

🌌 In other words, KYO is not just about knowing that there is an asteroid field ahead. It is about knowing which rocks are actually on your flight path, how big they are, and what controls are needed before the shields fail.

I look forward to unpacking more of MCO’s approach here, as this is an area of growing importance across the market.
